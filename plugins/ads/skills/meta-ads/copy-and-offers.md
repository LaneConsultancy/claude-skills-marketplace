# Ad Copy and Offer Framework

How to write the words on the ad. This is the structural framework for body copy, headlines, hooks, and CTAs. Pair with `andromeda-creative-system.md` (which covers creative *volume* and visual format) and the `facebook-ad-copy` skill (which generates audience-grounded variations using Digital Twins research).

### The Alignment Principle

Before writing a word, three elements must line up:

1. **A clear problem** the buyer feels right now
2. **A relevant offer** that solves that specific problem
3. **The right person** seeing the ad

Miss one and you're burning budget. For a plumber-website service: problem = "my site doesn't book jobs", offer = "high-converting site in 7 days", person = plumbing business owner. If any of those drift, the ad fails regardless of how good the copy is.

### Constructing a Killer Offer

A great offer is a **specific solution to a painful problem with a timeframe and a clear next step**. Vague language ("expert", "fast", "professional") signals nothing.

| Weak offer | Killer offer |
|------------|--------------|
| "We offer expert web design for plumbers. Call today." | "Is your website failing to book jobs? We'll build you a high-converting site in 7 days that turns visitors into calls. Message us for a free audit." |
| "Quality boiler installations across Kent." | "Boiler dead? New A-rated combi installed within 48 hours. Fixed price £1,895 — no surprises. Reply CALL for a same-day slot." |
| "Trusted local roofer." | "Roof leaking after the storm? Free 30-minute inspection within 24 hours, written quote on the spot. WhatsApp a photo to start." |

**Rule of tightness**: the more specific the timeframe, price, and next step, the better the ad performs. Force yourself to commit to numbers.

### The Hook–Problem–Offer–CTA Framework

Every body copy follows this four-beat structure:

**1. Hook (the scroll-stopper)** — first line, 3-7 words usually. Its only job is to stop the scroll.
- Do: lead with a pain point or curiosity ("Sick of shared leads that never pick up?")
- Don't: lead with yourself ("Hey [City] friends" / "We're a local family-owned business" / "Welcome to…"). Nobody cares about you yet — they care about their problem.

**2. Problem (the empathy beat)** — two sentences max. Show you understand the struggle.
- "Running a plumbing business is hard enough without a website that looks like it's from 2005."
- "You've already paid for shared leads that ghost you. Now you're paying again to chase them."

**3. Offer (the solution)** — your specific, low-pressure offer with timeframe. Same content as the killer-offer rules above.

**4. CTA (be a director, not a requester)** — tell them exactly what to do next.

| Bad CTA | Good CTA |
|---------|----------|
| "Contact us if interested." | "Message us for a quote in 30 seconds." |
| "Visit our website." | "Click 'Send Message' to get a price." |
| "Get in touch today!" | "Reply CALL and we'll ring you back in 10 minutes." |

### Headlines (the bold line under the image)

Two patterns work — pick one, don't try to do both:

- **Problem-focused**: "Website not booking calls?"
- **Result-focused**: "New site in 7 days"

Keep it under 8 words. If you can't decide, ship one of each as separate creatives — that's two of your 10-20 distinct concepts done.

### Tone: write like a person, not a corporation

- **Reading level: 5th-7th grade.** Test it — if a 12-year-old wouldn't understand a sentence, rewrite it.
- **Real words, short punchy sentences.** "Been in business 15 years. Still show up on time." beats "With over a decade and a half of industry experience, we pride ourselves on punctuality."
- **No corporate filler**: "leverage", "solutions", "synergy", "world-class", "passionate". These are red flags that an AI or junior copywriter wrote it.
- **Contractions on.** "We've", "you're", "won't" — that's how people speak.

### The 3 S Rule (sanity check before publishing)

Every ad must serve three reader behaviours:

1. **Scroll** — does the hook stop the thumb in the first 0.3 seconds? (visual + first line)
2. **Skim** — can someone get the offer in 2 seconds of scanning? (structure, line breaks, headline)
3. **Spark** — is there a specific action in the CTA that's easy to take right now?

If any of those fail, the ad will under-perform regardless of audience or budget.

### Short vs long copy: when to use which

| Use SHORT copy (1-3 lines) when… | Use LONG copy (5+ lines) when… |
|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| The image clearly shows the problem and result (before/after) | The offer needs trust-building or explanation |
| The offer is a commodity / well-understood category | High ticket (£1k+) or unfamiliar service |
| Retargeting warm traffic | Cold prospecting in a sceptical vertical |
| Lead form / WhatsApp / message CTA | Landing page CTA where the page does heavy lifting |

Default to short. Only go long when you can name a specific objection that needs handling in the copy.

### Expert shortcuts

**Stalk the winners — Meta Ads Library**
- Search competitors at [Meta Ad Library](https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/)
- Filter by country, category, and "active" status
- An ad that's been running 60+ days from a sophisticated competitor is almost always profitable — emulate its hook structure, visual treatment, and offer angle (don't copy the words; copy the *shape*)
- Pull 5-10 hooks from winning competitor ads as reference before briefing creative

**The Tweet Test**
- Before spending a penny, post your hook or headline as an organic tweet, LinkedIn post, or Instagram caption
- If it gets engagement (replies, saves, DMs) organically, it'll work as paid copy
- If it dies organically, the hook is weak — keep iterating before you spend

**The "say it out loud" test**
- Read the ad copy aloud to yourself
- If you'd be embarrassed to say it to a stranger in a pub, rewrite it
- This catches corporate filler faster than any rule

### Copy checklist (use before launching)

- [ ] Hook stops the scroll in <3 seconds, leads with their problem (not us)
- [ ] Problem named in their language (use Digital Twins — see `facebook-ad-copy`)
- [ ] Offer has a specific number (price, timeframe, or quantity)
- [ ] CTA tells them exactly what to do, not "get in touch"
- [ ] Headline is either problem-focused or result-focused, <8 words
- [ ] Reading level is 5th-7th grade (no corporate filler)
- [ ] 3 S Rule passes (scroll, skim, spark)
- [ ] Length matches the offer complexity (short by default)
- [ ] Audited Meta Ads Library for at least 3 sophisticated competitor ads
- [ ] Tweet test passed (if cold-prospecting a new angle)
