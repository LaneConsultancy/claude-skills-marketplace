## Step 2: Select an Ad Framework

Read the file at `~/.Codex/skills/youtube-video-ad/references/ad-frameworks.md` to review the full framework descriptions and best-fit scenarios.

The five available frameworks are:

1. **Number Hook** — Open with a compelling statistic or number. Stack 2–3 supporting benefits. Remove the primary risk or objection. Close with a direct CTA.
   - Best for: Products with a clear financial or measurable benefit. Works well when you have a genuine impressive number (savings figure, customer count, speed of delivery).

2. **Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)** — Open by identifying the audience's primary pain point. Agitate it — make them feel how bad it is. Then introduce the product as the relief.
   - Best for: Products that solve an obvious, emotionally charged problem. High-consideration purchases like boilers, insurance, healthcare.

3. **Before-After-Bridge (BAB)** — Show the current undesirable state. Show the desired future state. Position the product as the bridge between the two.
   - Best for: Transformation-oriented products. Home improvement, fitness, financial products.

4. **Social Proof Lead** — Open with a testimonial, review count, or impressive customer metric. Establish instant credibility before making any claims.
   - Best for: Markets where trust is the primary barrier. Trades, financial services, healthcare.

5. **Question Hook** — Open with a question so targeted and relevant that the audience cannot look away. The rest of the ad answers it.
   - Best for: Segmented audiences with a very specific, knowable problem. Niche B2B, health conditions, highly specific life situations.

Choose the framework that best fits the brief. If two frameworks are genuinely equally strong, briefly present both and let the user choose. Explain your recommendation in 1–2 sentences.

### VeO Prompt Engineering Rules

These rules are critical. Every VeO prompt you write must comply. A poorly written prompt will produce unusable clips.

**Anatomy of a strong VeO prompt — include ALL of these elements:**

1. **Subject** — Who or what is the primary focus?
2. **Action** — What are they doing? Be specific — not "a man stands" but "a man in his mid-50s crouches beside a newly installed combi boiler, running his hand along the pipework with quiet satisfaction"
3. **Style** — Photorealistic. Cinematic. Never specify "animation" or "illustration."
4. **Camera movement** — Always specify ONE of: static, slow zoom in, slow zoom out, handheld, tracking shot left/right, push in, pull back, aerial, tilt up, tilt down, arc left/right
5. **Composition** — Where is the subject in frame? Medium close-up, wide establishing, low-angle, over-the-shoulder, POV
6. **Lens / depth of field** — Shallow depth of field (bokeh background), deep focus, wide-angle, telephoto compression
7. **Ambiance / lighting** — Natural daylight through windows, overcast British light, warm interior incandescent, golden hour
8. **Audio direction** — Describe the sound the scene should produce. VeO 3.1 generates native audio — this is not optional. Examples below.

**Write 3–5 sentences per scene prompt. Be highly specific. Vague prompts produce generic clips.**

---

**Region authenticity (critical for conversion — viewers notice inauthenticity):**

- **UK scenes**: Red-brick terraced housing, British gas meters with dials, period sash windows, Victorian-era radiators, Artex ceilings, British-style kitchen fittings (worktop heights, sink configurations), overcast or muted natural light, GP-style waiting room aesthetics, British street furniture (post boxes, double-yellow lines), UPVC door frames with brass letter slots
- **US scenes**: American suburban ranch or colonial homes with front yards, US-style appliances (side-by-side fridge, American electrical outlets), oak or maple cabinetry, brighter more saturated interiors, manicured lawns visible through windows
- **EU scenes**: Apartment interiors with radiator covers, tiled floors, European-style electrical sockets, shuttered windows

Always name the setting explicitly in the prompt: "A mid-terrace Victorian home in East London" not "a British home."

---

**Native audio direction — how to write it:**

VeO 3.1 generates audio natively alongside video. The quality of the audio depends on how explicitly you describe it in the prompt. Include audio direction as the final sentence of every VeO prompt.

Examples of strong audio direction:
- `"The quiet, rhythmic hiss of a new combi boiler filling radiators, the house warming around it."`
- `"A notification chime and the tap of a smartphone screen as a homeowner opens a booking confirmation email."`
- `"A family at breakfast — clattering cutlery, cheerful morning chatter, the sound of a properly heated home."`
- `"The sound of a front door opening, a friendly Gas Safe engineer greeting a homeowner, footsteps on a wooden hallway floor."`

For dialogue/testimonial scenes, include quoted speech directly in the prompt — VeO will attempt to lip-sync it:
- `"A woman in her early 50s looks directly into camera and says, 'I didn't realise how much we were wasting until we switched. We've saved over eight hundred pounds this year.'"`

**Important**: Quoted dialogue in prompts is not always lip-synced perfectly. This is expected. Voiceover from ElevenLabs will be layered on top during assembly. The VeO audio serves as ambient sound bed.

---

**Negative prompts — what to exclude:**

Always include a `negativePrompt` field. Standard exclusions for every clip:

```
cartoon, anime, stylized, illustration, text overlay, watermark, logo, CGI, unrealistic, blurry, overexposed, grainy, drone footage (unless aerial is intended), studio backdrop, green screen
```

Add scene-specific exclusions as needed (e.g., for an interior scene: "exterior, outdoor, street").

## Step 2: Generate VeO Clips

### API Endpoint

```
POST https://generativelanguage.googleapis.com/v1beta/models/veo-3.1-fast-generate-preview:predictLongRunning
```

### Building the Full Prompt

For every scene, combine the Global Visual Direction and the scene-specific VeO prompt into one coherent prompt. Do not simply concatenate them with a line break — write them as a single, fluid instruction. The Global Visual Direction sets the stage; the scene prompt describes the specific action. They should read as one thing.

### Submitting a Generation Request

```bash
curl -s -X POST \
  "https://generativelanguage.googleapis.com/v1beta/models/veo-3.1-fast-generate-preview:predictLongRunning" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -H "x-goog-api-key: ${GEMINI_API_KEY}" \
  -d '{
    "instances": [{"prompt": "FULL_COMBINED_PROMPT_HERE"}],
    "parameters": {
      "aspectRatio": "16:9",
      "resolution": "1080p",
      "durationSeconds": "8",
      "personGeneration": "allow_adult",
      "negativePrompt": "cartoon, anime, stylized, text overlay, watermark, logo, CGI, unrealistic, blurry, overexposed",
      "seed": 42
    }
  }'
```

The response contains a `name` field — this is the operation name. Example: `"name": "operations/abc123xyz"`. Save this. You will poll it to check for completion.

### Polling for Completion

Poll every 10 seconds:

```bash
curl -s \
  "https://generativelanguage.googleapis.com/v1beta/OPERATION_NAME_HERE" \
  -H "x-goog-api-key: ${GEMINI_API_KEY}"
```

Check the response for `"done": true`. When the operation is done, extract the video URI from:

```
response.generateVideoResponse.generatedSamples[0].video.uri
```

If polling exceeds 6 minutes without completion, see the Error Handling section.

### Downloading the Clip

```bash
curl -s -o "youtube-ad-{slug}/clips/scene-{N}-v{V}.mp4" \
  "VIDEO_URI_HERE" \
  -H "x-goog-api-key: ${GEMINI_API_KEY}"
```

Use consistent naming: `scene-1-v1.mp4`, `scene-1-v2.mp4`, `scene-2-v1.mp4`. Never overwrite files.

**Important**: VeO-generated videos are stored for only 48 hours. Download immediately upon completion — never defer.

### Reference Frame for Visual Consistency

After the very first clip downloads successfully (scene 1, variation 1), extract a reference frame from its midpoint:

```bash
ffmpeg -i youtube-ad-{slug}/clips/scene-1-v1.mp4 \
  -ss 4 -vframes 1 -q:v 2 \
  youtube-ad-{slug}/clips/reference-frame.jpg
```

For all subsequent API calls, include this reference image in the request via the `referenceImages` parameter. This guides VeO to maintain visual consistency (lighting, colour grade, subject appearance) across all generated clips. Continue using `seed: 42` for all requests — this works in tandem with the reference frame.

### Parallel Generation Strategy

Do not wait for one clip to finish before submitting the next. Submit up to 4–5 generation requests concurrently, then poll all in-flight operations in round-robin.

Maintain a tracking table internally:

| Operation Name | Scene | Variation | Status | Output Path |
|---------------|-------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| operations/abc | 1 | 1 | polling | clips/scene-1-v1.mp4 |
| operations/def | 1 | 2 | polling | clips/scene-1-v2.mp4 |
| operations/ghi | 2 | 1 | polling | clips/scene-2-v1.mp4 |

When an operation completes: download it, run QC (Step 3), then submit the next queued request if any remain.

Report progress to the user as clips complete — don't wait for all clips to finish before communicating.

## Step 4: ElevenLabs Voiceover

Attempt voiceover generation for every scene that has VO text in the script. If any API call fails, skip that scene's VO silently, note it in the final report, and continue — the video will use VeO native audio for that scene.

### Generate Per-Scene VO Audio

The default voice is Rachel (`21m00Tcm4TlvDq8ikWAM`) — neutral, clear, professional English. Use this unless the script's Audio Direction specifies otherwise.

```bash
curl -s -X POST \
  "https://api.elevenlabs.io/v1/text-to-speech/21m00Tcm4TlvDq8ikWAM" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -H "xi-api-key: ${ELEVENLABS_API_KEY}" \
  -d '{
    "text": "VOICEOVER_TEXT_HERE",
    "model_id": "eleven_multilingual_v2",
    "voice_settings": {
      "stability": 0.5,
      "similarity_boost": 0.75,
      "style": 0.5,
      "speed": 1.0
    }
  }' \
  --output "youtube-ad-{slug}/audio/scene-{N}-vo.mp3"
```

**Voice settings guide for common briefs:**

| Tone | stability | similarity_boost | style | speed |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-------|-------|
| Warm / reassuring | 0.6 | 0.75 | 0.3 | 0.95 |
| Direct / authoritative | 0.5 | 0.75 | 0.5 | 1.0 |
| Energetic / urgent | 0.4 | 0.80 | 0.7 | 1.05 |
| Calm / measured | 0.7 | 0.70 | 0.2 | 0.90 |

### Concatenate Full VO Track

After all scene VO files are generated, concatenate them into a single track. Insert silence gaps between scenes — calculate gap duration as: `scene_start_time - previous_scene_end_time`.

```bash
# Generate a silence gap (replace 1.5 with the actual gap duration)
ffmpeg -f lavfi -i anullsrc=r=44100:cl=mono -t 1.5 \
  youtube-ad-{slug}/audio/gap-{N}.mp3

# Build a concat-list.txt file with all parts in order, then join
ffmpeg -f concat -safe 0 -i youtube-ad-{slug}/audio/vo-concat-list.txt \
  -c copy youtube-ad-{slug}/audio/full-vo.mp3
```

The `vo-concat-list.txt` file format:
```
file 'scene-1-vo.mp3'
file 'gap-1.mp3'
file 'scene-2-vo.mp3'
file 'gap-2.mp3'
...
```

## Step 5: FFmpeg Assembly

Before writing any assembly commands, read `~/.Codex/skills/youtube-video-ad/references/ffmpeg-commands.md` for pre-tested command patterns. Use those as your foundation. If the file is missing, use the commands documented here.

Work through these steps in sequence. Each step produces input for the next.

### Step 5a: Normalise All Selected Clips

Ensure every selected clip is H.264, 1920×1080, with AAC audio. Re-encode any that do not match:

```bash
ffmpeg -i youtube-ad-{slug}/selected/scene-{N}-selected.mp4 \
  -vf "scale=1920:1080:force_original_aspect_ratio=decrease,pad=1920:1080:(ow-iw)/2:(oh-ih)/2:color=black" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset fast -crf 18 \
  -c:a aac -b:a 192k \
  -pix_fmt yuv420p \
  youtube-ad-{slug}/selected/scene-{N}-normalised.mp4
```

### Step 5b: Trim Clips to Scene Duration

Each VeO clip is 8 seconds. Trim to the exact scene duration defined in the script:

```bash
ffmpeg -i youtube-ad-{slug}/selected/scene-{N}-normalised.mp4 \
  -t SCENE_DURATION_IN_SECONDS \
  -c copy \
  youtube-ad-{slug}/selected/scene-{N}-trimmed.mp4
```

If the script calls for a scene shorter than 1 second, pad to 1 second minimum using `-t 1.0`.

### Step 5c: Generate FFmpeg-Only Scenes

For CTA cards, title cards, or any scene flagged as `[FFmpeg generated]` in the assembly order:

```bash
# Solid dark card with centred white text
ffmpeg -f lavfi -i color=c=0x111111:s=1920x1080:d=SCENE_DURATION \
  -vf "drawtext=text='Visit UKboilers.org':
       fontfile=/System/Library/Fonts/Helvetica.ttc:
       fontsize=80:
       fontcolor=white:
       x=(w-tw)/2:
       y=(h-th)/2" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset fast -crf 18 -pix_fmt yuv420p \
  -c:a aac -ar 44100 -ac 2 \
  youtube-ad-{slug}/selected/scene-cta.mp4
```

Adjust the font path for the operating system. On Linux, use a path from `/usr/share/fonts/`. On macOS, use `/System/Library/Fonts/`. If the exact font is not available, use `fontfile=''` and FFmpeg will use its default — acceptable for a placeholder that will be replaced in post.

### Step 5d: Apply Inter-Scene Transitions

**For dissolve transitions** (where the script specifies "0.5s dissolve" or similar):

```bash
ffmpeg -i clip_A.mp4 -i clip_B.mp4 \
  -filter_complex \
  "[0][1]xfade=transition=dissolve:duration=0.5:offset=CLIP_A_DURATION_MINUS_HALF[v]; \
   [0:a][1:a]acrossfade=d=0.5[a]" \
  -map "[v]" -map "[a]" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset fast -crf 18 \
  -c:a aac -b:a 192k \
  dissolved_AB.mp4
```

Where `CLIP_A_DURATION_MINUS_HALF` = clip A's duration in seconds minus 0.5.

**For hard cuts**: no processing needed — handle via the concat approach in Step 5e.

**For fade to black**: use xfade with `transition=fade` to a black clip.

Apply transitions between each scene pair that requires one before building the concat list. Clips connected by hard cuts go directly into the concat list.

### Step 5e: Assemble the Full Video

Create `youtube-ad-{slug}/concat-list.txt`:

```
file 'selected/scene-1-trimmed.mp4'
file 'selected/scene-2-trimmed.mp4'
file 'selected/scene-3-trimmed.mp4'
file 'selected/scene-cta.mp4'
```

Concatenate:

```bash
ffmpeg -f concat -safe 0 -i youtube-ad-{slug}/concat-list.txt \
  -c copy \
  youtube-ad-{slug}/output/assembled-video-only.mp4
```

### Step 5f: Mix Audio Tracks

**If ElevenLabs VO was generated successfully:**

Lower VeO native audio to -15dB as an ambient bed. Layer ElevenLabs VO as the primary track. Mix them together:

```bash
ffmpeg -i youtube-ad-{slug}/output/assembled-video-only.mp4 \
  -i youtube-ad-{slug}/audio/full-vo.mp3 \
  -filter_complex \
  "[0:a]volume=-15dB[ambient]; \
   [1:a]volume=1.0[vo]; \
   [ambient][vo]amix=inputs=2:duration=first:dropout_transition=2[aout]" \
  -map 0:v \
  -map "[aout]" \
  -c:v copy \
  -c:a aac -b:a 192k \
  youtube-ad-{slug}/output/assembled-with-audio.mp4
```

**If no VO was generated (VeO native audio only):**

The assembled video already contains VeO audio. Use it directly at full volume. No mixing step required — `assembled-video-only.mp4` is your source for the final export.

### Step 5g: Final Export

Encode the final deliverable with web-optimised settings:

```bash
ffmpeg -i youtube-ad-{slug}/output/assembled-with-audio.mp4 \
  -c:v libx264 -preset slow -b:v 10M -maxrate 12M -bufsize 24M \
  -c:a aac -b:a 192k \
  -movflags +faststart \
  -pix_fmt yuv420p \
  youtube-ad-{slug}/output/final.mp4
```

The `-movflags +faststart` flag moves the MP4 metadata to the beginning of the file, enabling streaming before the full file downloads — required for YouTube uploads.

# ERROR HANDLING

Handle every error gracefully. Never crash out silently — always tell the user what happened and what you are doing about it.

| Error | Detection | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **VeO 429 — Rate Limited** | HTTP 429 response from API | Wait 60 seconds, retry. Maximum 3 retries. If still rate limited after 3 attempts, pause generation and inform user: "Rate limit persists — please wait 5 minutes then resume." |
| **VeO Polling Timeout (>6 min)** | Operation still not `done: true` after 360 seconds | Retry the same request using `veo-3.1-fast-generate-preview` (do NOT switch models — this is the only permitted model). Maximum 3 retries with 60-second waits between each. If all retries fail, generate an FFmpeg placeholder and note it for manual replacement. |
| **VeO Safety / Content Block** | Response contains `blocked: true` or safety category | (1) Remove any quoted dialogue from the prompt. (2) Remove specific brand names. (3) Simplify any action descriptions that could be ambiguous. Retry up to 3 times. If still blocked: generate an FFmpeg placeholder with scene label text and note it for manual replacement. |
| **VeO CRITICAL Quality — All Attempts Exhausted** | 3 attempts all received CRITICAL QC rating | Generate FFmpeg placeholder: solid colour card with scene number and label. Note clearly in final report: "Scene [N] — PLACEHOLDER. VeO could not produce an acceptable clip. Recommend manual filming or stock footage replacement." |
| **ElevenLabs API Error** | Any non-200 response from ElevenLabs | Log: "ElevenLabs voiceover unavailable for scene [N]. Using VeO native audio." Continue without VO for that scene. Do not retry automatically — ElevenLabs errors are usually quota/auth related. |
| **Download Failure** | `curl` returns empty file or HTTP error | Retry download 3 times with 5-second delays between each attempt. If all fail, request a new generation of that clip (re-submit to VeO API). |
| **FFmpeg Codec Mismatch** | `ffprobe` shows non-H.264 codec in a clip | Apply the normalise step (5a) to re-encode the offending clip to H.264 before including it in the concat list. |
| **FFmpeg Resolution Mismatch** | `ffprobe` shows resolution other than 1920×1080 | Apply scale + pad filter from step 5a to force 1920×1080 with black letterboxing/pillarboxing. |
| **FFmpeg Concat Failure** | Error during concat involving audio stream mismatch | Re-encode all clips (not just the offending one) through the normalise step to ensure identical audio parameters across all inputs. |
| **Reference File Missing** | File at `references/*.md` does not exist | Note what guidance is missing. Use the rules documented in this SKILL.md and proceed using best judgement. Do not stop the skill — the reference files supplement but do not replace this document. |
| **Working Directory Already Exists** | Directory `youtube-ad-{slug}` already exists | Warn the user: "Directory youtube-ad-{slug} already exists. Files may be overwritten. Continue? (yes/no)". Proceed only on confirmation. |

# TECHNICAL REFERENCE NOTES

These are facts about VeO 3.1 and the production pipeline that inform decision-making throughout the skill. Reference these when making judgement calls.

### VeO 3.1 Capabilities and Constraints

| Capability | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Native audio generation | VeO 3.1 generates audio natively alongside video — ambient sounds, dialogue, SFX, music |
| Text in video | Cannot generate readable text within frames. Any text VeO attempts will be blurry, inconsistent, or absent |
| Duration at 1080p | 8 seconds only — no shorter options at this resolution |
| Duration at 720p | Up to 148 seconds (for draft review only — not production quality) |
| Aspect ratios | 16:9 (standard) and 9:16 (vertical/Shorts) |
| Generation time | Typically 2–5 minutes per clip. May extend to 6 minutes. Timeouts after 6 minutes |
| Video storage | Generated clips stored by Google for 48 hours only — download immediately |
| Watermark | SynthID AI watermark applied automatically. Invisible to humans, does not affect ad performance |
| `personGeneration` for UK/EU | Must be `"allow_adult"`. Using `"allow_all"` in UK/EU regions may trigger safety blocks |
| Prompt language | English produces best results regardless of target audience region |
| Seed determinism | Same seed value increases visual consistency but does not guarantee identical outputs |
| Reference images | Passing a reference frame via `referenceImages` helps maintain colour, lighting, and style consistency across clips |

### Audio Mixing Strategy

The default production workflow:

1. **VeO generates** video + native ambient audio (sound bed)
2. **ElevenLabs generates** polished professional voiceover
3. **FFmpeg mixes**: VeO audio lowered to -15dB as ambient bed, ElevenLabs VO at full volume as primary track

Without ElevenLabs, VeO's native audio handles everything. For dialogue scenes, quoted speech in the VeO prompt can produce basic narration — quality varies, but it is acceptable for draft review.

### YouTube Ad Technical Requirements

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Maximum file size | 256 GB |
| Minimum resolution | 480p (1080p strongly recommended) |
| Codec | H.264 |
| Frame rate | 24, 25, or 30 fps (VeO outputs 24fps by default) |
| Audio | Stereo AAC |
| Container | MP4, MOV, AVI, or WMV |
| `-movflags +faststart` | Required for proper streaming behaviour on YouTube |

### Skippable vs. Non-Skippable In-Stream

| Format | Length | Skip |
|--------|--------|------|
| Skippable in-stream | 12 seconds to 6 minutes | Viewer can skip after 5s |
| Non-skippable in-stream | Up to 15 seconds | Cannot skip |
| Bumper ads | Exactly 6 seconds | Cannot skip |

The default script runtime of 27 seconds targets **skippable in-stream**. The first 5 seconds are critical — the hook must be compelling enough to retain viewers past the skip button.
