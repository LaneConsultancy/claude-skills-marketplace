# BUILD Mode Interview

Ask these questions **one at a time**. Do not batch. Wait for the answer before asking the next.

Skip any question the user has already answered in their initial request. Confirm what you have, then ask for the gaps.

## Required questions (all 7 must be answered before generating the brief)

### 1. Business + offer
> "What's the business, and what's the specific offer this landing page is selling?"

Listen for:
- A single offer, not the entire company catalogue. If they list 5 products, ask which one this page is for.
- The actual transaction (book a call, buy a product, request a quote, start a trial).

### 2. Target customer
> "Who is the customer this page is for? Get specific — not 'small business owners' but who exactly."

Listen for:
- Specific role/situation (e.g., "owner of a 5-15 person HVAC company in the US")
- Pain or trigger event (e.g., "just hit £1m revenue and stuck")
- What they currently use / current alternative

### 3. Dream outcome
> "What's the dream outcome — what does the customer actually want? Not your feature, their result."

Listen for:
- The result, not the mechanism ("more leads" not "Google Ads management")
- The emotional/status payoff if there is one ("be seen as the obvious choice in their market")
- Quantifiable if possible ("£10k extra revenue per month", "12 weeks to launch")

If they describe a feature, push: "And what does that get them? Keep asking 'so that' until you hit the real outcome."

### 4. Proof assets available
> "What proof do you have? List everything: text testimonials, video testimonials, case studies, before/after photos, screenshots of results, stats, press mentions, certifications, client logos, review counts."

Sort what they have into:
- **Strongest assets** → above the fold + main social proof section
- **Medium assets** → scattered throughout below the fold
- **Weak/missing assets** → flag in the brief as "need to create"

If they have nothing, flag immediately: "Without proof assets, this page will struggle. Can you collect [X] before we ship?"

### 5. Time to first result
> "How fast does the customer get the first meaningful result? Days, weeks, or longer?"

Listen for:
- The minimum viable win, not the full transformation
- E.g., "First booking in 7 days" not "Full system in 90 days"

This goes in the headline or subheadline if it's fast.

### 6. Risk reversal
> "What risk can you reverse? Money-back guarantee, free trial, no contract, no setup fee, free consultation, results guarantee, free until X?"

Listen for:
- The strongest reversal they can stomach
- Multiple stackable reversals (better)
- Why they're nervous about offering it (often surfaces objections worth addressing in copy)

If they refuse all risk reversal, push back: "30% conversion lift from badges under add-to-cart is documented. What's the actual blocker?"

### 7. Effort level
> "How much work does the customer have to do? Effortless (you do everything), light (they show up to calls), moderate (some homework), heavy (real time commitment)?"

This shapes:
- Subheadline ("without X, Y, Z" pattern if effortless)
- "How it works" section (3-4 steps must feel manageable)
- Objection handling in FAQ

## Optional clarifiers (ask if relevant)

### 8. Price point and pricing model
> "What does it cost, and how is it priced (one-off, subscription, by usage)?"

Affects whether pricing goes on the page or "book a call" replaces it.

### 9. Existing competitors / alternatives
> "Who else sells this? What do their landing pages do that you want to beat?"

Useful for headline differentiation. Don't go deep — this isn't market research, just sharp positioning.

### 10. Brand voice / tone
> "Any voice guidelines, or should I default to clear-direct-conversational?"

If they have a brand voice doc or specific tone, use it. Otherwise default to clear, direct, no jargon.

### 11. Build target
> "What stack will this be built in — React/Next, Astro, raw HTML, Webflow, WordPress, something else?"

Affects the hand-off recommendation at the end, not the brief itself.

## After the interview

Confirm back the seven required answers in 1-2 lines each before generating the brief. If anything is thin, push for one more sentence of detail rather than make it up.
