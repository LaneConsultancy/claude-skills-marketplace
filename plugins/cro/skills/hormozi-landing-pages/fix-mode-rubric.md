# FIX Mode Scoring Rubric

Score the existing landing page against six criteria, 0-10 each. Sum to a total out of 60.

For each criterion, write the score AND the specific reason. Generic scores ("looks fine, 7/10") are useless.

---

## 1. Above-the-fold clarity (0-10)

**The test:** A first-time visitor lands, looks for ≤3 seconds, then closes the tab. Can they explain back to you:
- Who this is for?
- What they get?
- Why they should care?

**Scoring:**

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | All three answers obvious. Dream outcome stated explicitly. Specific to the customer. |
| 7-8 | Two of three clear. One is implied but takes a second read. |
| 5-6 | One of three clear. The headline names what the company does, not what the customer gets. |
| 3-4 | Vague headline ("Smart software for modern teams"). Generic. No specificity. |
| 0-2 | Just the company name. Or a tagline that means nothing. No CTA above the fold. |

**Common failures:**
- "Welcome to [Company]"
- Headline names a feature ("AI-powered analytics") instead of an outcome ("Cut churn 40% in 90 days")
- No subheadline, or a subheadline that just repeats the headline
- CTA says "Get Started" or "Learn More" instead of a specific action

---

## 2. Value equation coverage (0-10)

**The test:** Does the page address all four components of Hormozi's value equation somewhere?
- Dream outcome (what they get)
- Perceived likelihood of success (why they'll believe it works)
- Time delay (how fast)
- Effort & sacrifice (how much work)

**Scoring:**

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | All four explicit. Dream outcome in headline, proof above and below fold, time qualifier in headline/subheadline, effort addressed in "how it works". |
| 7-8 | Three of four. One is weak or missing. |
| 5-6 | Two of four. Page leans heavily on features without addressing buyer psychology. |
| 3-4 | One of four. Page describes the product but doesn't sell it. |
| 0-2 | None addressed. Page reads like a brochure or "about us". |

**Common failures:**
- Heavy on features, light on outcomes
- No mention of how long it takes
- No proof anywhere
- Treats the customer like they already want it (no objection handling)

---

## 3. Headline strength (scannable as standalone) (0-10)

**The test:** Strip the page down to ONLY the headlines (h1, h2, h3). Read just those. Does the offer come through?

**Scoring:**

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Every section headline communicates value. Reading only headlines tells the full story. |
| 7-8 | Most headlines work standalone. 1-2 are weak ("How it works"). |
| 5-6 | Half the headlines are generic ("Features", "Benefits", "Testimonials"). |
| 3-4 | Most headlines are generic labels. They categorise but don't communicate. |
| 0-2 | All section headlines are filler. Nothing communicates without reading body copy. |

**Common failures:**
- "How it works" → should be "Three simple steps to [outcome]"
- "What our customers say" → should be the actual result ("£3m extra revenue in 12 months")
- "Features" → should be the benefit grouped by outcome
- "About us" → on a landing page, this section either earns its place or gets cut

---

## 4. Social proof quality (0-10)

**The test:** Is the proof visual, stacked, specific, and trustworthy?

**Scoring:**

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Multiple proof formats stacked (video + before-after + screenshot + text). Specific named results. Above fold AND throughout. Never hidden in carousel. |
| 7-8 | Strong proof but one weakness — maybe only one format, or hidden on mobile, or no above-fold trust strip. |
| 5-6 | Generic text testimonials only. Some are vague ("Great service!"). |
| 3-4 | One or two testimonials. No specificity. No visuals. |
| 0-2 | Zero proof. Or "trusted by industry leaders" with no logos. |

**Common failures:**
- Testimonial carousel that hides everything past slide 1
- "5-star rated" with no count or source
- Stock photo placeholder reviews
- Logo bar with logos the customer doesn't recognise
- Anonymous testimonials ("Sarah K." with no photo or context)

---

## 5. Risk reversal placement and strength (0-10)

**The test:** Is there a guarantee, badge, or risk-reversing signal under every CTA — not just the final one?

**Scoring:**

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Risk reversal under every CTA. Multiple stacked badges (3-5). Guarantee section explains it in full. |
| 7-8 | Risk reversal under primary CTAs but missing on secondary ones. Or guarantee mentioned but not visualised as badges. |
| 5-6 | One mention of a guarantee buried in the page. No badges. |
| 3-4 | Vague risk-reversing language ("trusted", "secure") but no actual reversal. |
| 0-2 | No risk reversal anywhere. Buyer takes 100% of the risk. |

**Common failures:**
- Guarantee in tiny text in the footer
- "30-day money-back guarantee" mentioned once and never visualised
- No badges under add-to-cart or book-a-call buttons
- The guarantee exists but isn't on the page

---

## 6. How-it-works simplicity (0-10)

**The test:** Is there a "how it works" section, and does it feel like 3-4 manageable steps — not 5+ that signal "this is work"?

**Scoring:**

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | 3-4 steps. Each step is a verb + outcome, one line. Section headline promises effortlessness. |
| 7-8 | 3-4 steps but one is wordy or feels heavy. Or section headline is generic ("How it works"). |
| 5-6 | 5 steps. Or 3-4 steps but each is a paragraph. |
| 3-4 | 6+ steps. Reads like onboarding documentation. |
| 0-2 | No "how it works" section. Or one that overwhelms the visitor. |

**Common failures:**
- "Step 1: Sign up. Step 2: Verify your email. Step 3: Complete onboarding. Step 4: Set up integrations. Step 5: Configure your team. Step 6: Launch." → that's 6 steps of work, not a sales pitch
- Each step is 3 sentences of explanation
- "How it works" section explains internal process, not customer experience

---

## Scoring totals

Sum across all six criteria (max 60).

| Total | Verdict | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 50-60 | Strong page | Polish only. Run AB tests on headline and hero image. |
| 40-49 | Working but leaking | Surgical fixes. Address Critical (above-fold) and Major (proof, risk) issues. |
| 25-39 | Significant rebuild | Either heavy fix list (10+ specific changes) or full rebuild. Recommend the rebuild if multiple criteria score under 5. |
| 0-24 | Rebuild from scratch | Do not surgically fix. Generate a BUILD brief instead, using the existing page only as research input. |

## When generating the fix list

For each criterion that scored under 7:
1. Quote the actual current copy or describe the current state (be specific)
2. Write the replacement copy or describe the structural fix
3. Estimate impact (Critical / Major / Polish)

Always order the output by impact, not by criterion number. Critical = above-fold fixes that affect what 100% of visitors see.

Never write "improve the headline". Always write the new headline.
