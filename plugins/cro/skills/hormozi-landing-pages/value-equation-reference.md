# Value Equation Reference

The full mapping of Alex Hormozi's value equation to landing page structure. Source: video transcript from Arsh Sanwarwala (ThrillX) breaking down Hormozi's strategy.

## The equation

```
              Dream outcome × Perceived likelihood of success
  Value = ────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                  Time delay × Effort & sacrifice
```

To increase perceived value, you either:
- **Increase the numerator** (better outcome, more believable)
- **Decrease the denominator** (faster, easier)

On a landing page, every section can be evaluated against which equation lever it pulls. If a section doesn't pull any lever, cut it.

---

## 1. Dream outcome (numerator — increase)

**Where it lives on the page:**
- Primary headline (above the fold)
- Section headlines throughout the page
- Final CTA block headline
- Hero image (visual representation of the outcome)

**Rules:**
- Specific, not generic. "Cut energy bills 40%" beats "Lower bills".
- Customer's outcome, not your feature. "Get found on Google" not "SEO services".
- Visual + verbal. The hero image should show the outcome the headline promises.

**The "so that" principle:**

Most companies write headlines that name what they do. Hormozi's fix: chain "so that" clauses until you hit the actual dream outcome.

Example from the transcript (solar panel cleaning):
- Original headline: "Exterior cleaning services"
- Apply "so that": "We offer exterior cleaning **so that** your solar panels produce more energy **so that** you cut your energy bill **so that** you feel confident your solar is working"
- Final headline: takes the last/best "so that" clause as the actual headline
- Result: 64% conversion lift

**How to apply:**
1. Write what you do (the boring version)
2. Add "so that..." and complete the sentence
3. Repeat until you hit a real human outcome
4. Use the last clause (or the strongest one) as your headline

**Hero image guidance:**
- Headline promises a luxury closet → image shows a beautiful luxury closet
- Headline promises a brighter smile → image shows the brighter smile
- Headline promises smoother skin → image shows the smoother skin
- NEVER use an abstract product shot when you can show the outcome

## 2. Perceived likelihood of success (numerator — increase)

**Where it lives on the page:**
- Trust strip above the fold (logos, stars, customer count)
- Main social proof section
- Proof scattered throughout below the fold
- Risk reversal under every CTA
- Guarantee section

**Two levers to pull:**

### Lever 2a: Social proof

Hormozi's quote: "Your proof is going to do more selling than any promise can possibly do."

**Rules:**
- **Visual > text.** Text: "I lost 20lb". Better: before/after photo. Best: weighing-in video.
- **Stack proof.** One testimonial = forgettable. 50 testimonials in a wall = overwhelming, sold.
- **Never hide in carousel.** Carousels have low engagement. What's hidden doesn't get seen.
- **Specific results.** "£3m revenue in 12 months" beats "great service".
- **Multiple formats.** Stack videos + before-afters + text reviews + screenshots together.

**The wall-of-love format:**
- Mosaic grid of proof — videos, images, screenshots, text reviews
- All visible at once, especially on mobile
- Overwhelming volume = trust without reading individual items
- "Subconsciously build so much trust that they're already sold"

### Lever 2b: Risk reversal

Hormozi: "If I buy stuff and they guarantee it, or they make it risk-free, or they show me tons of proof, I'm more likely to pay for it and pay more for it."

**Formats:**
- Money-back guarantee (30-day, 60-day, lifetime)
- Free trial (no card required is stronger)
- No contract / cancel anytime
- Warranty (lifetime > 10-year > 1-year)
- Free installation / free setup
- Results guarantee ("we don't deliver, you don't pay")

**Placement rules:**
- Under EVERY CTA, not just one
- 3-5 small badges with icons works best
- Expand into a full guarantee section once on the page
- Documented impact: 30% conversion lift from badges under add-to-cart

## 3. Time delay (denominator — decrease)

**Where it lives on the page:**
- Headline qualifier
- Subheadline
- "How it works" section step labels
- Sometimes in section headlines below the fold

**Rules:**
- Be specific with the timeframe
- Highlight the minimum viable result, not the full transformation
- Use the timeframe in the headline if it's compelling

**Examples from the transcript:**
- Online mediation course: "Build a six-figure mediation practice in less than 30 days"
- E-com supplement: "[Result] in just 2 weeks"
- Fractional CFO: "Get your first big financial win in just 7 days"

**Frame the minimum win:**
- Don't say: "Full transformation in 90 days"
- Say: "See your first result in 7 days, full transformation in 90"
- The 7 days is what gets them to convert

**When time is long:**
If your service genuinely takes months/years, lean on a milestone:
- "Booked your first client in 14 days" (even if full client base takes 12 months)
- "First savings hit your account in 30 days" (even if full results take 2 years)

If you genuinely can't promise a fast first win, this lever is weak — push harder on the other three.

## 4. Effort & sacrifice (denominator — decrease)

**Where it lives on the page:**
- Subheadline ("without X, Y, Z" pattern)
- "How it works" section (3-4 steps MAX)
- FAQ (handles objections about effort)
- Anywhere you can say "we do this so you don't have to"

**Subheadline pattern from the transcript:**
> Headline: "Launch a high-paying career or business fully debt free"
> Subheadline: reduces effort by listing what the customer GETS (not has to do): "personal success coach, 24/7 tutoring, hands-on skill training, lifetime career support"

The subheadline qualifies the headline with effort-reducing context. "Without X, Y, Z" is one valid pattern. "We give you [supports]" is another. Both signal: this won't be hard.

**The "how it works" rule:**
- 3-4 steps. NOT 5. NOT 6.
- Past 4 steps, the perceived effort goes up
- If you genuinely have 7 steps, group them into 3 phases
- Each step: verb + outcome, one line
- Section headline must promise effortlessness: "Three simple steps", "Get [outcome] in [time]"

**Common effort-reducers to feature:**
- "Done for you" services
- "No technical setup required"
- "Works with what you already have"
- "Cancel anytime"
- "We migrate your data"
- "30-minute setup"
- "No coding required"

## How to use this reference

When generating a brief or audit, for every major section ask:
1. Which equation lever does this pull?
2. How can it pull harder?

If a section pulls no lever, cut it. A landing page is not a brochure.

The four levers in priority order (where to focus first):
1. **Dream outcome** in the headline — biggest impact, hardest to get right
2. **Perceived likelihood** above the fold + throughout — builds the trust to act
3. **Effort & sacrifice** in subheadline + how-it-works — removes "this is too much work" objection
4. **Time delay** as headline qualifier where possible — accelerates the decision

If you can pull all four levers on the same page, you've built a Hormozi-grade landing page.
