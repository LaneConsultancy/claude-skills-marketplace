# Research Prompts for Local Pages

Perplexity research prompt templates to gather subject matter expertise before writing.

## How to Use

Before writing any service or location page, run a Perplexity research query to gather:
- Industry-specific knowledge
- Common customer questions
- Credentials and accreditations
- Pricing context
- Differentiating factors

Use `mcp__perplexity__perplexity_research` with the templates below.

---

## Generic Research Prompt Template

Use this template for any industry:

```
Research the following for a local business website:

Topic: [SERVICE TYPE / INDUSTRY]
Location: [LOCATION - typically UK]
Target audience: [WHO NEEDS THIS SERVICE]

Provide:

1. CUSTOMER PAIN POINTS
   - What problems drive people to seek this service?
   - What frustrations do they have with current providers?
   - What are they worried about when hiring someone?

2. COMMON QUESTIONS
   - What questions do customers typically ask?
   - What should they ask but often don't?
   - What information helps them make a decision?

3. CREDENTIALS & QUALIFICATIONS
   - What professional registrations are required or expected?
   - What trade associations matter?
   - What manufacturer certifications add credibility?
   - UK-specific requirements

4. PRICING CONTEXT
   - Typical price ranges for common jobs
   - What factors affect pricing?
   - How is pricing typically structured (hourly, fixed, etc.)?
   - UK market context

5. QUALITY DIFFERENTIATORS
   - What separates good providers from bad ones?
   - What red flags should customers watch for?
   - What questions expose a provider's quality?

6. SEASONAL / TIMING FACTORS
   - Are there busy seasons for this service?
   - When should customers book in advance?
   - Emergency vs. planned work considerations

7. COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS
   - What do customers often get wrong?
   - What myths exist in this industry?
   - What should they know but usually don't?

Focus on specific, factual information that demonstrates expertise.
Provide UK-specific context where relevant.
```

## Using Research Results

After running the research query:

1. **Extract Key Facts**
   - Specific credentials to mention
   - Price ranges for context
   - Common problems to address
   - Questions to answer in FAQ

2. **Identify Proof Points**
   - Credentials that matter
   - Standards to reference
   - Differentiators to highlight

3. **Shape Content Sections**
   - "Common problems" section topics
   - FAQ questions
   - Trust signals to include

4. **Avoid Regurgitating**
   - Use facts to inform writing
   - Don't copy research verbatim
   - Transform into persuasive copy

---

## Research Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
- Research before writing every page
- Extract specific facts and figures
- Use research to demonstrate expertise
- Verify credentials mentioned are current/relevant

### Don't:
- Copy research text directly into pages
- Include irrelevant industry information
- Overwhelm pages with technical details
- Forget to localise for UK context
