# Copywriting Rules for Local Pages

Apply these principles to every service page and location page.

## Core Philosophy

**Advertising is salesmanship in print.** — John E. Kennedy

Every piece of copy must do what a good salesman does: give people reasons to buy. Pretty words, clever phrases, and "brand awareness" without selling power is waste.

---

## The Reason-Why Framework

**Every claim must have a reason-why.** Don't just state benefits — prove them.

| Weak (No Reason-Why) | Strong (With Reason-Why) |
|----------------------|--------------------------|
| "We provide excellent service" | "We arrive within 2 hours because we're based locally in Greenhithe and carry common parts in our van" |
| "Trusted by homeowners" | "4.9 stars from 127 Google reviews — here's what Mrs Thompson from Dartford said last week..." |
| "Quality workmanship" | "12-month warranty on all parts because I only fit manufacturer-approved components" |
| "Fast response times" | "Same-day service for most calls because I limit my jobs to 3 per day" |
| "Competitive prices" | "Fixed pricing quoted before work starts — no hidden extras or call-out fees" |

### How to Add Reason-Why

For every claim you write, ask: **"Why should they believe this?"**

Then add:
- **Specifics** — numbers, timeframes, amounts
- **Mechanism** — how you deliver on the promise
- **Proof** — credentials, reviews, testimonials
- **Guarantee** — what happens if you fail

---

## The Rule of One

Every page, every section has ONE dominant idea:

- One core promise
- One primary emotion
- One clear call to action

Multiple ideas dilute the message. Find the single most compelling angle and commit to it fully.

**For a service page:** What's the ONE thing that makes you different?
**For a location page:** What's the ONE reason locals should choose you?

---

## Customer Awareness Levels

Match your approach to where the reader is:

| Level | What They Know | Your Approach |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Most Aware | Know you, ready to buy | Lead with offer, make it easy |
| Product Aware | Know solutions exist, unsure about you | Build trust, show proof |
| Solution Aware | Know outcome wanted, not your solution | Educate on your method |
| Problem Aware | Feel the pain, don't know solutions | Empathise first, then reveal solution |
| Unaware | Don't know they have a problem | Lead with story or bold claim |

**For local service pages:** Most readers are Problem Aware or Solution Aware. They have a problem (broken boiler) and are comparing solutions. Lead with empathy, then differentiate.

---

## Specificity = Trust

The more specific you are, the more believable you become.

| Generic (Low Trust) | Specific (High Trust) |
|--------------------|-----------------------|
| "Years of experience" | "15 years in the trade, 8 serving Dartford" |
| "Qualified engineer" | "Gas Safe registered (#123456)" |
| "Great reviews" | "4.9 stars from 127 Google reviews" |
| "Local service" | "Based in Greenhithe, 10 minutes from Dartford centre" |
| "Quick response" | "Usually with you within 2 hours" |
| "Affordable prices" | "Typical boiler repair: £85-£150 including parts" |

---

## 2026 Context: The Trust Gap

The market is flooded with AI-generated "slop." High-performing copy must feel aggressively human, specific, and transparent.

**"Boring Copy" Wins:**
- Replace adjectives with data
- Replace promises with mechanisms
- Specificity = trust

**Bad:** "Revolutionize your heating with our expert services."
**Good:** "I'll diagnose the fault, quote a fixed price, and in most cases have your heating back on the same day."

---

## Voice Guidelines

Write like a knowledgeable neighbour, not a corporation:

**Do:**
- First person where appropriate ("I" or "we")
- Contractions (I'm, you'll, we've)
- British English spelling
- Plain language
- Honest about limitations
- Genuinely helpful

**Don't:**
- Corporate jargon ("solutions", "leverage", "synergy")
- Passive voice ("services are provided")
- Buzzwords ("cutting-edge", "revolutionary", "world-class")
- False urgency ("Act now!", "Limited time!")
- Vague superlatives ("best in class", "second to none")

## The Lead (First 100-150 Words)

The opening determines whether they keep reading. Get it right.

### Structure for Local Service Pages

1. **Acknowledge their situation** (1-2 sentences)
   - Show you understand their problem

2. **State your promise** (1-2 sentences)
   - What you'll do for them

3. **Establish credibility** (1-2 sentences)
   - Why you can deliver

4. **Soft next step** (1 sentence)
   - What happens next

### Example Lead

> When your boiler breaks down in the middle of winter, you need it fixed today — not next week. I'm a Gas Safe registered engineer based in Greenhithe, and I can usually be with you within 2 hours.
>
> I'll diagnose the problem, give you a fixed price before I start, and in most cases have your heating running again the same day. If I can't fix it, you don't pay a call-out fee.

## Proof Elements

Include at least 3 proof elements per page:

1. **Credentials**
   - Professional registrations (Gas Safe, NICEIC, etc.)
   - Trade association memberships
   - Manufacturer accreditations

2. **Social Proof**
   - Review stats (X stars from Y reviews)
   - Named testimonials with location
   - Case studies

3. **Guarantees**
   - Workmanship warranty
   - Satisfaction guarantees
   - Price promises

4. **Experience**
   - Years in trade
   - Jobs completed
   - Area-specific experience

5. **Specifics**
   - Response times with mechanism
   - Pricing examples
   - Process details

---

## Calls to Action

Every page needs ONE clear call to action.

### CTA Formula

**[Action] + [Outcome] + [Friction Reducer]**

Examples:
- "Call now for same-day repair — free diagnosis included"
- "Get a fixed-price quote — no obligation, no call-out fee"
- "Book online — we'll confirm within 2 hours"

### CTA Section Content

Include:
- Primary contact method (usually phone)
- Alternative method (form, email)
- What happens when they contact you
- Reassurance (no obligation, etc.)

---

## Writing Checklist

Before delivering any page:

### Persuasion
- [ ] Is there one clear message/promise?
- [ ] Does every claim have a reason-why?
- [ ] Are specifics used instead of generalities?
- [ ] Is there social proof (reviews, credentials)?
- [ ] Is the call-to-action clear and easy?

### Voice
- [ ] Does it sound like a real person?
- [ ] Are corporate buzzwords eliminated?
- [ ] Is the language appropriate for the audience?
- [ ] Would you say this face-to-face?

### Trust Signals
- [ ] Credentials mentioned with registration numbers?
- [ ] Review stats or testimonials included?
- [ ] Guarantees explained with specifics?
- [ ] Local presence established?
