# Copywriting Skill

Write effective copy by combining classic principles with modern best practices.

## Core Philosophy

**Advertising is salesmanship in print** — John E. Kennedy

Every piece of copy must do what a good salesman does: give people reasons to buy. Pretty words, clever phrases, and "brand awareness" without selling power is waste.

## 2026 Context: The Trust Gap

The market is flooded with AI-generated "slop." High-performing copy must feel aggressively human, specific, and transparent.

**"Boring Copy" Wins:**
- Replace adjectives with data
- Replace promises with mechanisms
- Specificity = trust

**Bad:** "Revolutionize your workflow with AI."
**Good:** "Cut data entry time from 10 hours to 10 minutes using automated SQL scripts."

**For B2B:** Remember buyers practice "Defensive Decision-Making" — they're protecting their jobs, not just seeking ROI. Write for the committee, not just your champion.

## The Reason-Why Framework

Every claim must have a reason-why. Don't just state benefits — prove them.

**Weak:** "We provide excellent service"
**Strong:** "We arrive within 2 hours because we're based locally in Greenhithe and carry common parts in our van"

**Weak:** "Trusted by homeowners"
**Strong:** "4.9 stars from 127 Google reviews — here's what Mrs Thompson from Dartford said last week..."

## The Rule of One

Every page, every section has ONE dominant idea:
- One core promise
- One primary emotion
- One clear call to action

Multiple ideas dilute the message. Find the single most compelling angle and commit to it fully.

## Customer Awareness Levels

Match your approach to where the reader is:

| Level | What They Know | Your Approach |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Most Aware | Know you, ready to buy | Lead with offer, make it easy |
| Product Aware | Know solutions exist, unsure about you | Build trust, show proof |
| Solution Aware | Know outcome wanted, not your solution | Educate on your method |
| Problem Aware | Feel the pain, don't know solutions | Empathise first, then reveal solution |
| Unaware | Don't know they have a problem | Lead with story or bold claim |

## Lead Selection (First 100-300 Words)

The lead determines 80% of your success. Choose based on awareness:

**Direct Leads (High Awareness):**
- Offer Lead — "Get X for £Y"
- Promise Lead — "The fastest way to..."
- Problem-Solution — "Struggling with X? Here's why..."

**Indirect Leads (Low Awareness):**
- Secret Lead — "What most people don't know about..."
- Proclamation Lead — Bold claim that demands attention
- Story Lead — Narrative that draws reader in

See `references/lead-types.md` for detailed frameworks and examples.

## Writing Structure

### Headlines
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Promise a benefit or provoke curiosity
- Be specific (numbers, timeframes, locations)
- Never sacrifice clarity for cleverness

### Body Copy
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- One idea per paragraph
- Use subheadings every 150-250 words
- Include proof throughout (stats, testimonials, specifics)
- Address objections before they arise

### Calls to Action
- Clear, specific, action-oriented
- Reduce friction (what happens next)
- Reinforce the benefit
- Make it easy (phone, booking link, form)

## Voice Guidelines

Write like a knowledgeable neighbour, not a corporation:
- First person where appropriate
- Contractions (I'm, you'll, we've)
- British English spelling
- No jargon or corporate speak
- Honest about limitations
- Genuinely helpful, not salesy
