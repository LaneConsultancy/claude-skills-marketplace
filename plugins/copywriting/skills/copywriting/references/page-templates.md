# Page Templates & Ogilvy Principles

Practical templates for common page types, informed by David Ogilvy's proven techniques.

## Ogilvy's Core Principles Applied

### On Headlines

**"On the average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy."**

Your headline is 80 cents of your dollar. If it doesn't work, everything that follows is wasted.

**Headlines that work:**
- Promise a benefit
- Contain news
- Include specific information
- Appeal to self-interest

**Headlines that fail:**
- Clever wordplay that obscures meaning
- Vague generalities
- Questions without implied answers
- Anything that could apply to any competitor

### On Proof

**"The customer is not a moron. She's your wife."**

Readers are intelligent and sceptical. They've been advertised to their entire lives. You cannot trick them — you must convince them with evidence:

- Specific numbers over vague claims
- Named testimonials over anonymous quotes
- Demonstrated results over promised benefits
- Your credentials over your self-praise

### On Simplicity

**"I write as if I was sitting in a chair beside the reader."**

Write at the reading level of your audience (usually lower than you think). Short sentences. Short paragraphs. Simple words.

**Replace:**
- "utilise" → "use"
- "facilitate" → "help" or "make easy"
- "in order to" → "to"
- "at this point in time" → "now"

### On Being Boring

**"You cannot bore people into buying your product. You can only interest them in buying it."**

But "interesting" doesn't mean entertaining or clever. It means relevant, specific, and useful to someone actively looking for what you offer.

The most interesting thing to a homeowner with a broken boiler is: "I can fix it today for £X."

## Service Page Template

**Target length:** 600-1000 words

### Structure

```
[H1: Primary keyword + benefit/differentiator]

[Lead: 100-150 words addressing the reader's situation and your core promise]

[H2: What we do / How it works]
[Clear explanation of the service, 150-200 words]

[H2: Why choose us / What makes us different]
[Your key differentiators with proof, 150-200 words]
- Bullet points work well here
- Include specific numbers or guarantees
- Reference reviews or credentials

[H2: Common questions OR Specific situations we handle]
[Address objections or demonstrate expertise, 150-200 words]

[H2: Areas we cover] (for local services)
[List of locations, brief, can include schema]

[H2: Get in touch / Book now / Next steps]
[Clear CTA with contact details, 50-100 words]
```

### Example Opening (Boiler Repair)

> ## Same-Day Boiler Repairs in Dartford
>
> When your boiler breaks down, you need it fixed fast — not three days from now. I'm a Gas Safe registered engineer based in Greenhithe, and I can usually be with you within 2 hours for emergency repairs.
>
> I'll diagnose the problem, give you a fixed price before starting work, and in most cases, have your heating back on the same day. No call-out charges if I can't fix it.

## Homepage Template

**Target length:** 400-700 words (focused, not exhaustive)

### Structure

```
[H1: What you do + primary location + key differentiator]

[Hero section: 50-75 words summarising your core promise]

[Social proof: Review snippet or key credential]

[H2: Core services overview]
[Brief descriptions linking to full service pages]

[H2: Why local customers choose us]
[Key differentiators with proof, brief]

[Testimonial block: 1-2 featured reviews]

[H2: Covering [Area]]
[Brief location statement]

[CTA section: Contact information, booking link]
```

### Homepage Lead Example

> ## Your Local Heating Engineer in Dartford & North Kent
>
> Boiler repairs, servicing, and installations from a Gas Safe registered engineer you can actually get hold of. Same-day emergency call-outs. Fixed prices. No excuses.
>
> ⭐ 4.9 stars from 127 Google reviews

## Location Page Template

**Target length:** 1000-1500 words

For targeting specific towns/areas. Each location page should comprehensively cover all services offered in that area, with dedicated sections for each service.

### Structure

```
[H1: Primary Service + Location]

[Lead: 100-150 words. Location-specific opening that establishes local presence and credibility. Reference local landmarks, neighbourhoods, or area characteristics where possible.]

[H2: [Service 1] in [Location]]
[250-350 words covering this specific service. Include:
- What the service involves
- Common situations/problems addressed
- Your approach and what customers can expect
- Reason-why proof (credentials, experience, guarantees)]

[H2: [Service 2] in [Location]]
[250-350 words following same pattern]

[H2: [Service 3] in [Location]]
[250-350 words following same pattern]

[H2: [Service 4] in [Location]] (if applicable)
[250-350 words following same pattern]

[Local testimonial block if available]

[H2: Areas we cover near [Location]]
[50-100 words listing nearby towns, postcodes, neighbourhoods]

[CTA section with contact details, 50-75 words]
```

### What Each Service Section Should Include

Each 250-350 word service section should cover:

1. **What the service involves** — explain the work in plain terms
2. **Common situations addressed** — when/why someone would need this
3. **Your approach** — what makes your method effective or different
4. **Reason-why proof** — credentials, experience, guarantees, or specifics

**Example structure:**

> ## [Service] in [Location]
>
> [Opening sentence: what this service is and why it matters to the reader]
>
> [Paragraph explaining what's involved — the process, what customers can expect, how long it typically takes. Written in plain English, not technical jargon.]
>
> [Paragraph covering your approach and expertise. Include specific credentials, years of experience, or particular specialisms. Add a guarantee or promise if relevant.]
>
> [Closing that addresses common concerns or variations. Mention different types/situations you handle to demonstrate breadth of expertise.]

**The key is substance:** each section should contain enough real information that a reader feels informed, not just reassured by vague promises.

### Key Principles for Location Pages

**Make each page genuinely useful:**
- Provide real information about each service, not thin filler content
- Write as if this is the only page a visitor might read
- Include enough detail that someone could make an informed decision to contact you

**Add local relevance where possible:**
- Reference local areas, neighbourhoods, or landmarks naturally
- Include local testimonials if you have them
- Mention any area-specific considerations (e.g., older housing stock, common boiler types in the area)

**Avoid:**
- Simply swapping the town name across duplicate pages
- Making promises about specific response times
- Generic content that could apply to any location
- Thin service descriptions that don't inform the reader

## Blog Post Template

**Target length:** 800-1500 words

### Structure

```
[H1: Benefit-focused title including keyword]

[Lead: Problem statement or hook, 100-150 words]

[H2: Main content section 1]
[250-400 words]

[H2: Main content section 2]
[250-400 words]

[H2: Main content section 3] (if needed)
[250-400 words]

[H2: Conclusion / What to do next]
[Summary with natural CTA, 100-150 words]
```

### Blog Post Principles

**Educational content should:**
- Answer a genuine question people search for
- Provide actionable information
- Establish your expertise through demonstration
- Link naturally to relevant services (not forced)

**Avoid:**
- Thin content written only for keywords
- Generic advice available everywhere
- Obvious sales pitches disguised as education
- Content that doesn't help the reader

## The Pre-Publish Checklist

### Persuasion
- [ ] Is there one clear message/promise?
- [ ] Does every claim have a reason-why?
- [ ] Are specifics used instead of generalities?
- [ ] Is there social proof (reviews, credentials)?
- [ ] Is the call-to-action clear and easy?

### Voice
- [ ] Does it sound like a real person?
- [ ] Are corporate buzzwords eliminated?
- [ ] Is the language appropriate for the audience?
- [ ] Would I say this face-to-face?

### SEO
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1, first 100 words, meta title?
- [ ] Headings structured logically (H1 → H2 → H3)?
- [ ] Meta description written for clicks?
- [ ] Internal links to relevant pages?
- [ ] Local signals included (for service businesses)?

### Technical
- [ ] Reads well on mobile?
- [ ] Images optimised and have alt text?
- [ ] Contact details visible?
- [ ] Forms/buttons working?
