# Anti-AI Writing Guide for Copywriters

Write human from the first draft. Don't write AI slop and fix it later — internalise these rules and produce text that never triggers detection in the first place.

## The Non-Negotiables

### 1. Never Use These Words/Phrases

These are instant AI detection flags. Banish them from your vocabulary:

**Single words:** delve, harness, leverage, robust, seamless, cutting-edge, game-changer, elevate, streamline, foster, facilitate, synergy, holistic, paradigm, empower, innovative, transformative, optimize, scalable, ecosystem, stakeholders, disruptive

**Phrases:** "In today's fast-paced world", "navigate the complexities", "it's important to note", "at the end of the day", "a testament to", "best-in-class", "actionable insights", "thought leadership", "value proposition", "Whether you're a... or a..."

**Transitions:** "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally", "In conclusion"

Instead: use plain English. "Use" not "leverage". "Solid" not "robust". "Also" not "Furthermore". If a word sounds like it belongs in a LinkedIn post from 2023, replace it.

### 2. Break the Rhythm from the Start

Don't write sentences of uniform length. Deliberately vary:
- Follow a 25-word sentence with a 4-word one
- Use a one-sentence paragraph for impact
- Drop in the occasional fragment. Like this.
- Ask a question to break the flow
- Let a thought run long sometimes, connecting clauses with dashes and commas, the way people actually think when they're working through an idea

### 3. Have an Opinion

AI hedges. Humans commit.

**AI:** "There are several approaches that may be beneficial depending on your specific circumstances."
**Human:** "Honestly? Go with option B. The others work in theory but I've seen them fall apart in practice."

Every piece of copy should have at least one clear opinion, preference, or honest qualifier.

### 4. Be Specific, Not Sweeping

**AI:** "We provide excellent service to numerous satisfied customers across the region."
**Human:** "We've done 340 roofs across Kent since 2019. 127 Google reviews, 4.9 stars. Last week we re-tiled a Victorian terrace in Dartford — took three days, came in at £3,200."

Specifics are the fastest shortcut to sounding human. AI can't invent real details.

### 5. Write How People Actually Talk

- Use contractions (we're, don't, it's, that's)
- Start sentences with "And", "But", "So"
- Use dashes for mid-thought shifts — like this
- Include asides in parentheses (real people have secondary thoughts)
- Say "about" not "approximately", "use" not "utilise", "get" not "obtain"

## Calibration by Content Type

| Type | Personality Level | Key Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Blog / email | High | Full voice, opinions, asides |
| Landing page | Medium-High | Personality serves conversion |
| Service page | Medium | Warm + professional |
| B2B content | Low-Medium | Authority with subtle warmth |

## Quick Self-Check

Before finishing any piece of copy, scan for:

- [ ] No AI phrases from the kill list above
- [ ] Sentence lengths genuinely vary (check — really look)
- [ ] At least one opinion, aside, or honest qualifier
- [ ] Specifics instead of vague claims where possible
- [ ] Transitions are natural, not formulaic
- [ ] The read-aloud test: would you actually say this?

If the copy could have been generated by any AI chatbot with zero context about the business, it's not done yet.
