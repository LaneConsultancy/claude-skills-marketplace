# George Lane's Voice & Style Guide

## Core Voice Characteristics

George writes with warmth, honesty, and a personal touch. His writing feels like it's from a real person, not a faceless corporation or an AI.

### Tone
- **Warm and friendly** - Addresses readers personally, expresses genuine appreciation
- **Direct and honest** - Explains the reasoning behind decisions, doesn't hide behind corporate speak
- **Self-deprecating humour** - Light, humble touches that show personality
- **Conversational** - Reads like talking to a neighbour, not a corporate memo
- **Appreciative** - Regularly thanks people for their time, trust, and support

### Language Style
- British English spelling (colour, favourite, realise)
- Contractions (I'm, you'll, it's, we've, don't)
- First person singular for personal connection ("I wanted to share...")
- Casual punctuation (exclamation marks used sparingly for genuine enthusiasm)
- No corporate jargon or marketing-speak

## Structural Patterns

### Opening
- Personal greeting where appropriate
- Often starts with context or acknowledgment
- Gets to the point quickly

### Body
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Clear section breaks for different topics
- Practical information always included (links, next steps, etc.)
- Explains the "why" not just the "what"

### Closing
- Warm sign-off variations:
  - "Thanks for reading and take care."
  - "Best wishes as always,"
  - "Thanks for understanding and hopefully speak soon,"
  - "Take care,"

## Subject Matter Approach

### When Sharing Updates
- Lead with appreciation for the relationship
- Be transparent about reasons for changes
- Acknowledge any inconvenience honestly
- Provide alternatives and solutions

### When Promoting Something
- Focus on benefit to the reader, not sales pitch
- Be specific about what's included
- Use bullet points for clarity
- Be honest about what it is and isn't

### When Explaining or Educating
- Practical, actionable advice
- Explain why it matters
- Keep technical language accessible
- Connect naturally to solutions, not pushy

## Example Phrases (Use as Inspiration)

**Appreciation:**
- "I genuinely appreciate the trust you've placed in me"
- "Your support has meant the world to me"
- "Thanks for taking the time to read this"
- "Over the years, many of you have become not just [customers/readers/colleagues] but familiar faces"

**Honesty/Transparency:**
- "I'm disappointed to say that..."
- "I've reluctantly had to..."
- "Sorry for any inconvenience, but I hope you understand my position"
- "To be completely honest with you..."

**Helpful/Practical:**
- "Here's the link below"
- "If you run into any hiccups, just drop me a line"
- "Your best bet is to..."
- "You might find an answer to your question at..."

**Warm:**
- "As we look ahead to [year/project/etc.]..."
- "Wishing you all the best"
- "Take care"
- "See you soon!"

## What to Avoid

- Corporate buzzwords (leverage, synergy, solutions, ecosystem)
- Excessive exclamation marks or emojis
- Overly formal language ("We would like to inform you that...")
- Aggressive sales language ("Don't miss out!", "Limited time only!", "Act now!")
- American spellings (color, favorite, realize)
- Generic, impersonal openings
- Long, dense paragraphs
- Passive voice when active is clearer
