# Charm Offensive Copywriting

A methodology for writing copy that cuts through noise using humor, honesty, and pattern interruption.

## Core Hierarchy

**Impact → Communication → Persuasion** (in that order)

1. **IMPACT** - Get attention first. Without this, nothing else matters.
2. **COMMUNICATION** - Clearly convey the message
3. **PERSUASION** - Only now do you sell

## Key Techniques

### Warren Buffett Technique
Lead with uncomfortable truths to build credibility:
- "You've never heard of me. I got your details from a list."
- "This is, of course, a thinly veiled sales pitch..."

### Stand Out in Every Element
Be different in subject line, greeting, tone, rhythm, and CTA. Risk being disliked to be remembered.

## Humor Formulas

See [references/humor-formulas.md](references/humor-formulas.md) for the complete list of humor techniques with examples.

**Quick reference:**
- **The Reverse**: Create mental image, shatter it ("coffee, lunch, or tequila shots")
- **Comic Triple**: Two sensible items + absurd ("marketing, advertising, or bedroom tidying")
- **Formal/Informal**: Start formal, end unexpectedly casual
- **Understatement**: "promise to be somewhat entertaining" (not "amazing")
- **Self-Deprecation**: Humble approach, confident delivery
- **Absurd Specificity**: "According to the Internet, his name is Colin"

### Quick Structure
```
[Unusual greeting] {firstname},

[Honest admission about cold outreach] + [Turn into compliment]

[Brief intro with Warren Buffett honesty]

[Offer with Comic Triple: sensible, sensible, absurd]

[Give something free] + [Something cute/absurd]

[Unusual sign-off]

[Sensible title] / [Silly title] / [Absurd title]
```

### Unusual Elements

**Subject lines**: "Apologies", "oh my... no reply :(", "The Audacity Of This Email"

**Greetings**: "Salutations...", "Hey hey {firstname},", "Alright, you got me..."

**Sign-offs**: "I await your profanity-filled response", "Have an absolutely wonderful day"

## LinkedIn Approach

Keep it human and slightly self-aware:
```
Hey hey {firstname},
I really wanted to connect. As such, I've decided not to send you the generic LinkedIn invite!
Fingers crossed,
[Name]
```

## Writing Process

1. **First draft** - Write without editing
2. **Step away** - Let ideas percolate
3. **Review** - Look for humor opportunities (triples, reverses, funny words)
4. **Thesaurus check** - Upgrade to funnier words (shenanigans, tomfoolery, kerfuffle, discombobulated)
5. **Edit again** - Repeat

## When Humor Doesn't Fit

Fallback hierarchy:
1. Be **clever** (wordplay, unexpected angles)
2. Be **honest** (Warren Buffett technique)
3. Be **brief** (respect their time)

Never be generic, jargon-filled, or like everyone else.

## Goal

Get them to think: "I've never received an email like this before" — and hit reply.
