# Email Templates

## The "Drunk Cold Email" Wireframe

```
[Unusual greeting] {firstname},

You've never heard of me. (Hi, I'm X.) I got your details from [some source]. *gasp!* But hey, at least [turn this into a compliment somehow.]

Ok. Let me get down to brass tacks. I'm writing to you because I run [very brief introduction.]

We're looking to win clients over from those [describe competitors in extreme way, then retract.]

If you let me have a chat with you about your [service 1], [service 2], [absurd service 3] needs, I'll take you for [sensible option 1], [sensible option 2], [ridiculous option 3] and promise to be [understated benefit]. If you're lucky, I may even [do something silly]. First off, I'd like to [give them something for free.]

I have included [something cute/absurd/funny]. According to the Internet, [needless detail.] I trust this [will have desired effect.]

I await your [angry/baffled/stern but fair] response.

Have an absolutely wonderful day,

[Your name]
[Sensible title], [silly title 1], [silly title 2]
```

## Subject Lines

Instead of typical sales subjects:
- "Apologies" (intriguing)
- "oh my... no reply :(" (vulnerable)
- "I messed up..." (honest)
- "The dreaded break up email" (dramatic)
- "ok... you got me, {firstname}" (personal)
- "The Audacity Of This Email" (self-aware)
- "I've accepted it..." (curious)

## Greetings

Avoid "Hi" or "Hello":
- "Salutations..."
- "Greetings..."
- "Hey hey {firstname},"
- "Alright, you got me, {firstname}..."
- "Oh no, {firstname},"

## Sign-offs

- "Have an absolutely wonderful day"
- "I await your profanity-filled response"
- "Fingers crossed,"
- "[Sensible title] / [Silly title] / [Absurd title]"

## Follow-Up Sequence: The 5 Stages of Grief

A proven follow-up framework using the 5 stages of grief as a narrative device:

**Email 1 - DENIAL:**
"How could {firstname} resist my unsolicited sales message? They must not have received it..."

**Email 2 - ANGER:**
"Why did I think sending a weird email was a clever idea? Damn it. You've ruined it, {yourname}!"

**Email 3 - BARGAINING:**
"Please. I'll do anything. I'll do you a free {X} on the house..."

**Email 4 - DEPRESSION:**
"I can't do anything. 'Email {firstname},' they said. I guess I'm not cut out for this sales stuff..."

**Email 5 - ACCEPTANCE:**
"I get it. You weren't at all charmed by my opening gambit... Time to pour myself a large glass of whisky and write sad poetry about the deal we never did."

**Email 6 - THE BREAKUP:**
"I've run out of stages of grief. I guess it's time for the infamous 'break up email'... *sniff*"

**Playful unsubscribe humor:**
- "This link also releases the hounds on me"
- "Turns the air conditioning on full blast in my house"

## LinkedIn Templates

### Connection Request
```
Hey hey {firstname},
I really wanted to connect. As such, I've decided not to send you the generic LinkedIn invite!
Fingers crossed,
[Name]
```

### One-Two Punch (Lead Gen)

**Invite:**
```
Nice to 'meet' you. I have a weird skill that means I can book meetings with just about anybody.
If you're at all curious, click 'accept'. :)
If not, send me your most cutting insult. :(
```

**Follow-up after accept:**
```
Thanks for not sending me a cutting insult!
If you're curious about the ideas I mentioned, let me know and I'll spill the beans.
I say 'ideas'... It is of course, a thinly veiled sales pitch. However, I often get too enthusiastic and give away great ideas. You should take advantage of this!
```

## Exercises for Developing Voice

### 1. The Letter of Hate
Write an angry rant about something that annoys you (jargon, bad customer service, boring emails). Extract the funny lines and honest observations. Polish these into copy.

### 2. The Honest Bio
Write about yourself with brutal honesty. Include flaws. Make it self-deprecating but confident. This becomes your "ambitious underdog" story.

### 3. The Productive Rant
When something annoys you, channel it into copy that lampoons the target.
