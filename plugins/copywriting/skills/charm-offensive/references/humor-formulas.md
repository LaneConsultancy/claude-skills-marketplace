# Humor Formulas

## 1. The Reverse
Create a mental image, then shatter it with an unexpected twist.

**Structure:** [CREATE MENTAL IMAGE] → [SHATTER MENTAL IMAGE]

**Examples:**
- "I'll take you for coffee, lunch, or tequila shots"
- "Your digital marketing, advertising, or bedroom tidying needs"
- "I await your profanity-filled response"
- "Director / Interim Head of Stationery / Apparent Top Hat Connoisseur"

## 2. The Comic Triple (Rule of Three)
Two sensible items followed by something absurd.

**Structure:** [SENSIBLE] - [SENSIBLE] - [SILLY]

**Examples:**
- "coffee, lunch, or tequila shots"
- "data, copy, or interpretive dance"
- "marketing, advertising, or bedroom tidying"

## 3. Triple Reverse (Combined)
Use the Rule of Three AND The Reverse together.

**Example:** "If you let me chat about your marketing, sales, or hamster grooming needs, I'll take you for coffee, lunch, or an ill-advised trip to Vegas..."

## 4. Formal/Informal Pattern
Start with formal language, end with unexpected informality.

**Examples:**
- "Salutations..." (unusual formal greeting)
- "I await your profanity-filled response"
- "Have an absolutely wonderful day" (sincere but slightly over-the-top)

## 5. Understatement (Litotes)
Say less than expected for comic effect.

**Examples:**
- "promise to be somewhat entertaining" (not "amazing" or "hilarious")
- "not as good" instead of "terrible"
- "I may even wear a top hat" (understated absurdity)

## 6. Immediate Retraction
Say something bold, then immediately walk it back.

**Examples:**
- "those evil (ok maybe not evil... but not as good) other agencies"
- "This groundbreaking... well, it's not groundbreaking. But it's quite good."

## 7. Self-Deprecation with Confidence
Be humble about your approach while remaining confident.

**Examples:**
- "I've accidentally been clever"
- "I trust this will charm you into submission"
- "I hope this doesn't make you think I'm a weirdo. Well, I am. But a professional one."

## 8. Absurd Specificity
Add oddly specific but irrelevant details.

**Examples:**
- "According to the Internet, his name is Colin"
- "This button releases the hounds on me"
- "A large novelty-sized hand slaps me around the face with impressive force"

## 9. Edited Cliché
Take a familiar phrase and twist it.

**Examples:**
- "I await your profanity-filled response" (vs. "I look forward to hearing from you")
- "Grief-stricken, even..." (playful exaggeration)

## 10. Putting Words in Their Mouth
Imagine what they're thinking and voice it humorously.

**Example:** "How can it be?! How could {firstname} resist the urge to respond to my completely unsolicited sales message?"

## Funny Words to Consider

Words that sound inherently amusing:
- Shenanigans, tomfoolery, kerfuffle
- Cajolement, bamboozle, flummox
- Peculiar, befuddled, discombobulated
- Tequila, ferret, novelty-sized
