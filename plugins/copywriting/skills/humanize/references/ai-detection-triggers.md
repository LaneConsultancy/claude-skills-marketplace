# AI Detection Triggers — Complete Reference

## How AI Detection Works

AI detectors analyse text using four main signals:

1. **Statistical predictability (perplexity):** AI-generated text is highly predictable — each word follows the most likely next word. Detectors measure this. Low perplexity = probably AI.
2. **Vocabulary patterns:** AI reaches for the same pool of "safe" corporate words. Detectors maintain lists of these and flag text that leans on them heavily.
3. **Structural uniformity:** AI produces eerily consistent paragraph lengths, sentence lengths, and organisational patterns. Real humans don't write like that.
4. **Absence of human quirks:** AI text lacks the rough edges of human writing — sentence fragments, tangents, abrupt shifts in tone, casual asides, contradictions, personality.

The goal of humanisation is to break ALL four signals simultaneously. Fixing just one (e.g., swapping a few words) won't fool modern detectors. You need unpredictable vocabulary, varied structure, genuine personality, and the occasional rough edge.

## Structural Patterns That Trigger Detection

### Uniform Sentence Length

**Problem:** AI tends to write sentences of similar length (15-25 words). Human writing has wild variation.

**Fix:** Deliberately alternate. A 30-word sentence. Then four words. Then something in between. Then a question? Then back to a longer thought that meanders a bit before landing.

### Perfect Paragraph Structure

**Problem:** AI writes paragraphs of 3-4 sentences, each roughly the same length, with clear topic sentences.

**Fix:** Use 1-sentence paragraphs. Use 6-sentence paragraphs. Start a paragraph mid-thought. End one abruptly.

### Symmetrical Lists

**Problem:** AI loves balanced lists (3 items of similar length, parallel structure).

**Fix:** Make lists uneven. One item gets a longer explanation. Another is just three words. Break the pattern.

### Over-Hedging

**Problem:** AI says "may", "could", "it's possible that", "in some cases" constantly.

**Fix:** Take a position. "This works." not "This may potentially work in certain circumstances."

### The Summary-Sandwich

**Problem:** AI structures content as: overview → details → summary. Every time.

**Fix:** Start with a detail. Jump to the conclusion. Circle back with context. Or just tell it straight without the summary bookends.

### Excessive Signposting

**Problem:** AI tells you what it's about to tell you, tells you, then tells you what it told you.

**Fix:** Just tell people things. Trust the reader. "Here are 5 ways to..." → just list them.

## Perplexity and Burstiness

**Perplexity** measures how predictable text is. AI text has LOW perplexity (very predictable). Human text has HIGHER perplexity (surprising word choices, unexpected turns).

**To increase perplexity:**
- Use unexpected synonyms occasionally
- Break expected sentence structures
- Include domain-specific or colloquial vocabulary
- Insert the occasional tangent or aside

**Burstiness** measures variation in sentence complexity. AI text has LOW burstiness (consistent complexity). Human text has HIGH burstiness (simple sentences mixed with complex ones).

**To increase burstiness:**
- Follow complex sentences with dead-simple ones
- Mix technical language with casual language in the same piece
- Vary paragraph length dramatically (1 sentence to 8 sentences)

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Business Description

**AI version:** "We are a leading provider of innovative roofing solutions, dedicated to delivering exceptional quality and seamless customer experiences. Our team of skilled professionals leverages cutting-edge techniques to ensure robust, long-lasting results for every project."

**Human version:** "We fix roofs. We've been doing it for 15 years across Kent, and we're good at it — 4.9 stars on Google from 127 reviews. We use modern materials and proper techniques, but honestly, what matters most is that we show up when we say we will and don't leave a mess."

### Example 2: Email Opening

**AI version:** "I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out to discuss an exciting opportunity that could significantly benefit your organisation. In today's competitive landscape, it's crucial to stay ahead of the curve."

**Human version:** "Quick one — I noticed your team is hiring three new developers, which usually means the existing codebase is becoming a bottleneck. We helped a similar-sized company (Acme Corp) cut their deployment time from 4 hours to 20 minutes last quarter. Thought it might be relevant."

### Example 3: Service Description

**AI version:** "Our comprehensive gutter cleaning service is designed to provide homeowners with peace of mind. We utilise state-of-the-art equipment and industry-leading techniques to ensure your gutters remain in optimal condition, preventing costly water damage and maintaining the structural integrity of your property."

**Human version:** "Blocked gutters are one of those things you don't think about until water's running down your wall. We clean them out properly — not just scooping the leaves but checking the brackets, testing the downpipe flow, and clearing any moss that's built up on the fascia. Takes about an hour for a standard semi. £65."
