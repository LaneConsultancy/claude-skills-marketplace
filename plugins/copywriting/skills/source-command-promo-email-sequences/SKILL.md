## Command Template

# Promo Email Sequence Skill

Write multi-email promotional campaigns that convert — using proven direct response architecture, not manipulative countdown tricks.

## Core Philosophy

A good email sequence is a conversation, not a countdown timer. Each email must earn the next open. The sequence works because every email delivers genuine value while building toward the offer — not because you're shouting "LAST CHANCE" progressively louder.

**Principles:**
- Every email must be worth reading even if they never buy
- Proof before promises, stories before statistics
- Urgency must be real (genuine deadlines, limited spots, price increases) — never manufactured
- The sequence should feel like a trusted mate giving you the heads up, not a used car salesman cornering you

## Before Writing Any Sequence

Ask the user for:
1. **What's the offer?** — Product, service, price, any bonuses
2. **Who's the audience?** — What do they already know? What's their awareness level?
3. **What's the proof?** — Results, testimonials, case studies, data
4. **What's the real deadline?** — Is there a genuine reason for urgency?
5. **What's the transformation?** — Before/after for the customer
6. **Sequence length?** — Default to 5 emails over 7 days unless specified

If the user hasn't provided these, ask before writing. Bad inputs produce bad sequences regardless of structure.

## Sequence Architecture

### The 5-Email Standard Sequence (7 Days)

This is the workhorse. It handles most product launches, course openings, service promotions, and seasonal campaigns.

```
Day 1: THE PROOF BOMB (Credibility + Announcement)
Day 2: THE STORY (Transformation narrative)
Day 4: THE OBJECTION KILLER (Address resistance + bonus stack)
Day 6: THE DEADLINE APPROACH (Social proof + urgency)
Day 7: THE FINAL CALL (Last chance — morning + evening if appropriate)
```

### The 7-Email Extended Sequence (7-10 Days)

For higher-ticket offers or colder audiences that need more warming.

```
Day 1: THE PROOF BOMB (Credibility + Announcement)
Day 2: THE STORY (Transformation narrative)
Day 3: THE DEEP DIVE (Method/mechanism reveal)
Day 4: THE OBJECTION KILLER (Address resistance + bonus stack)
Day 5: THE SOCIAL PROOF PILE (Results roundup)
Day 7: THE DEADLINE APPROACH (Scarcity + urgency)
Day 7 (PM): THE FINAL CALL (Closing argument)
```

### The 3-Email Flash Sequence (48 Hours)

For flash sales, time-sensitive offers, or re-engagement of warm leads.

```
Email 1: THE ANNOUNCEMENT (Offer + reason why now)
Email 2: THE REMINDER (12-24hrs later — proof + objection handling)
Email 3: THE CLOSE (Final hours — transformation focus)
```

## Email-by-Email Breakdown

### Email 1: THE PROOF BOMB

**Purpose:** Establish credibility so hard that the reader trusts everything that follows.

**Structure:**
- Open with a specific, verifiable result (revenue figure, transformation, measurable outcome)
- Brief context — how/why this matters to THEM
- Introduce the offer as the logical next step
- Keep the pitch light — this email sells the NEXT email as much as the offer
- CTA: Soft — "here's the link if you want to look" not "BUY NOW"

**Key principle:** Lead with your single most impressive proof point. Not three decent ones — ONE knockout.

**Subject line style:** Specific result or intriguing claim. Numbers work well here.

### Email 2: THE STORY

**Purpose:** Make the transformation feel real and achievable through narrative.

**Structure:**
- Tell a story — yours, a customer's, or a composite
- Follow the Before > Struggle > Discovery > After arc
- Make the "before" relatable to the reader's current situation
- Show the method/offer as the bridge between before and after
- CTA: Still relatively soft — link to offer or reply with questions

**Key principle:** The reader should see themselves in the story. If they can't, the story is wrong for this audience.

### Email 3: THE DEEP DIVE (Extended sequence only)

**Purpose:** Explain the mechanism — WHY this works, not just THAT it works.

**Structure:**
- Address the "how does this actually work?" question
- Reveal enough of the method to build confidence
- Don't give away everything — show the map, not every step of the journey
- Position the offer as the shortcut/implementation
- CTA: Medium — encourage questions or direct to offer

**Key principle:** Specificity builds trust. Vague claims feel like marketing. Concrete mechanisms feel like education.

### Email 4: THE OBJECTION KILLER

**Purpose:** Remove every reason NOT to buy.

**Structure:**
- Directly address the top 3-5 objections (price, time, "will it work for me?", trust)
- Stack bonuses here if they exist (adds perceived value at the decision point)
- Use FAQ format or conversational objection-handling
- Include guarantee/risk reversal
- CTA: Direct — "if those were your concerns, they're handled — here's the link"

**Key principle:** The objections you don't address are the ones that kill the sale. Be honest about limitations too — it builds trust faster than pretending they don't exist.

### Email 5: THE SOCIAL PROOF PILE (Extended sequence only)

**Purpose:** Overwhelm doubt with evidence from real people.

**Structure:**
- Stack testimonials, case studies, results screenshots
- Variety matters — different industries, experience levels, use cases
- Let customers' words do the selling
- Brief commentary connecting each proof point back to the reader
- CTA: Direct — "join them"

**Key principle:** One testimonial is a claim. Five testimonials are a pattern. Ten testimonials are a movement.

### Email 6: THE DEADLINE APPROACH

**Purpose:** Create genuine urgency while respecting the reader.

**Structure:**
- Acknowledge the deadline honestly ("closing in 24 hours because...")
- Quick recap of the strongest proof point and the transformation
- Address the "I'll do it later" objection directly
- New angle or previously unmentioned benefit
- CTA: Clear and urgent — specific time/date the offer ends

**Key principle:** Real deadlines work. Fake ones destroy trust. If you don't have a real deadline, create one (limited intake, genuine price increase, seasonal relevance) or don't use urgency at all.

### Email 7: THE FINAL CALL

**Purpose:** Last opportunity — clear, concise, no fluff.

**Structure:**
- Short — this is not the time for a 1,000-word essay
- Recap the offer in 2-3 sentences
- Final proof point or emotional appeal
- Clear deadline with specific time
- CTA: Unmistakable

**Key principle:** By this point, they've read the story, seen the proof, had their objections addressed. This email is just the nudge. Keep it tight.

## Subject Line Principles

Each email needs a subject line that earns the open without resorting to clickbait.

| Email | Subject Line Approach | Example |
|-------|----------------------|---------|
| Proof Bomb | Specific result | "£14k from a single email sequence" |
| Story | Curiosity/narrative | "She needed a car in 7 days" |
| Deep Dive | Education/mechanism | "Why most promo emails get ignored" |
| Objection Killer | Direct address | "The 3 reasons you haven't signed up yet" |
| Social Proof | Social evidence | "What 47 business owners said" |
| Deadline | Honest urgency | "[Closing tomorrow] Quick recap" |
| Final Call | Clear finality | "Last call — doors close at midnight" |

**Rules:**
- Never use ALL CAPS for entire subject lines
- Brackets sparingly and honestly — [Closing tomorrow] not [URGENT ACTION REQUIRED]
- Personalisation ({firstname}) only if the list supports it
- Preview text matters as much as subject line — write both together

## Tone & Voice

### Do
- Write like you're talking to someone you respect
- Use contractions, short sentences, line breaks
- Be specific (numbers, dates, names)
- Show genuine enthusiasm without being manic
- Acknowledge the reader's intelligence
- Be honest about what the offer ISN'T good for

### Don't
- Use pressure tactics that rely on fear or shame
- Write every sentence in bold with exclamation marks!!!
- Make claims you can't back up
- Use "Dear valued subscriber" energy
- Pile on false scarcity ("only 3 spots left" when there are 300)
- Send more than 2 emails in a single day (morning + evening on final day max)

## Adapting for Different Offers

### Service-Based (Trades, Consulting, Local)
- Emphasise availability, local proof, speed of results
- Urgency = limited diary slots, seasonal demand
- Proof = specific job outcomes, Google reviews, before/after

### Digital Products (Courses, Templates, Software)
- Emphasise transformation, community, support
- Urgency = cohort start date, early bird pricing, bonus expiry
- Proof = student results, demo content, testimonials

### Physical Products (E-commerce)
- Emphasise limited stock, seasonal relevance, value
- Urgency = genuine stock levels, shipping cutoffs
- Proof = reviews, unboxing, usage photos

## Writing Process

1. **Map the sequence** — Decide which template fits (3, 5, or 7 emails)
2. **Gather proof** — Collect all testimonials, results, data before writing
3. **Write Email 1 first** — If the proof bomb doesn't land, nothing else matters
4. **Write the Final Call second** — Know where you're heading
5. **Fill the middle** — Story, objections, deep dive
6. **Write subject lines last** — Once you know each email's strongest angle
7. **Read aloud** — Every email should sound like a person talking, not marketing copy

## Template Wireframes

### Email 1: THE PROOF BOMB — Wireframe

```
Subject: [Specific number/result that makes them stop scrolling]
Preview: [Context that makes the subject line even more compelling]

[Opening line — the proof point. One sentence. Specific. Verifiable.]

[1-2 sentences of context — why this matters to them, not to you]

[Brief intro of what made this result possible — the offer, positioned as
a natural consequence not a hard sell]

[What's inside / what they get — 3-5 bullet points max, benefits not features]

[Soft CTA — "Have a look here if you're curious" energy, not "BUY NOW OR MISS OUT"]

[Sign-off — warm, personal]

P.S. [Either a secondary proof point or a teaser for tomorrow's email]
```

**Example openings:**
- Trades: "Last month, a plumber in Essex added £4,200 to his monthly revenue by automating one thing — his follow-up process."
- Digital: "I wrote 5 emails last Tuesday. They brought in £31k by Sunday. Here's the thing — I didn't actually write them."
- Service: "We fitted 47 boilers in January. The year before, in the same month, we fitted 19. Same team, same prices, same area."

### Email 2: THE STORY — Wireframe

```
Subject: [Curiosity-driven — the story hook]
Preview: [Sets up the narrative without giving away the ending]

[Open with the "before" — paint the picture of someone in the reader's
situation. Make it specific and relatable.]

[The struggle — what they tried that didn't work. 2-3 sentences max.]

[The turning point — what changed. This is where your offer/method
enters the story naturally.]

[The "after" — specific results. Numbers, feelings, tangible changes.]

[The bridge — "This is exactly what [offer] helps you do"]

[CTA — slightly warmer than email 1. "If [character]'s situation sounds
familiar, this might be worth a look"]

[Sign-off]
```

**Story arc formula:**
- Relatable character (could be you, a customer, a composite)
- Specific problem (not vague "struggling with business")
- Failed attempts (builds empathy — "I tried X, Y, Z")
- Discovery moment (your method/offer enters)
- Concrete result (transformation with proof)

### Email 3: THE OBJECTION KILLER — Wireframe

```
Subject: [Directly addresses the biggest objection]
Preview: [Honest framing — "fair question, here's the answer"]

[Open by naming the objection — "You might be thinking..."]

[Address it head-on with proof, logic, or reframing]

[Second objection — address with a different angle]

[Third objection — "will it work for MY situation?"]

[Bonus stack (if applicable) — positioned as removing risk,
not just adding value]

[Guarantee / risk reversal — make the "worst case" feel safe]

[CTA — direct. "If those were your holdups, they're handled.
Here's where to go"]

[Sign-off]
```

**Common objection reframes:**
| Objection | Reframe |
|-----------|---------|
| "Too expensive" | Cost of NOT solving the problem + ROI math |
| "Not enough time" | Time saved once implemented + quick start guide |
| "Will it work for me?" | Similar person testimonial + guarantee |
| "I can figure it out myself" | How long that'll take vs. shortcut |
| "I've been burned before" | What makes this different + risk reversal |
| "I need to think about it" | What happens while you're thinking |

### Email 4: THE DEADLINE APPROACH — Wireframe

```
Subject: [Honest time reference — closing tomorrow / 24 hours left]
Preview: [Quick recap of what's at stake]

[Short opening — "Quick one today."]

[Recap the offer in 2-3 sentences — what they get, what it does]

[Strongest proof point — repurposed or new]

[Name the deadline and WHY it's real]

[Address the "I'll do it later" excuse — what changes if they wait?]

[CTA — clear, urgent, specific time]

[Sign-off — brief]
```

**Real urgency vs. fake urgency:**
- Real: Cohort start dates, genuine price increases, limited diary slots, seasonal relevance, bonus expiry
- Fake: "Only 5 spots left" (unlimited digital), evergreen countdown timers, "Price going up" with no actual change planned

### Email 5: THE FINAL CALL — Wireframe

```
Subject: [Clear finality — "Last call" / "Closing tonight"]
Preview: [Exact deadline time]

[2-3 sentences max. No preamble.]

[What: The offer, one line]
[Why: The transformation, one line]
[When: Exact closing time]
[Where: Link]

[Optional: One final emotional appeal or proof point — 1-2 sentences]

[CTA — unmistakable]

[Sign-off — warm but brief]

P.S. [Exact time it closes. That's it.]
```

**This email should be SHORT.** Anyone reading email 5 has already read 1-4. They don't need more information — they need a nudge.

### 3-Email Flash Sequence Wireframes

**Flash Email 1: THE ANNOUNCEMENT**
```
[What the offer is — straight in, no lengthy preamble]
[Why now — genuine reason for the timing]
[What they get — 3 bullets max]
[When it ends — specific date/time]
[CTA]
```

**Flash Email 2: THE REMINDER** (12-24hrs later)
```
[Quick reminder + one proof point they haven't seen]
[Address the #1 objection in 2-3 sentences]
[Updated deadline]
[CTA]
```

**Flash Email 3: THE CLOSE** (Final 2-4 hours)
```
[Short. Direct. "Closing in X hours."]
[One sentence on the transformation]
[Link]
[Done.]
```

## Sequence Spacing Guidelines

| Sequence Length | Recommended Spacing |
|----------------|-------------------|
| 3 emails | Day 1, Day 1+12hrs, Day 2 |
| 5 emails | Day 1, Day 2, Day 4, Day 6, Day 7 |
| 7 emails | Day 1, Day 2, Day 3, Day 4, Day 5, Day 7, Day 7 (PM) |

**Rules:**
- Never more than one email per day until the final day
- Final day can have morning + evening (announce this: "one more tonight, then I'll shut up")
- Leave at least one gap day in 5+ email sequences — silence builds anticipation
- Send times: generally 7-9am for opens, 12-2pm for engagement, 6-8pm for final calls

## Adapting Tone by Industry

### Trades & Home Services
- Direct, practical language
- Focus on reliability and trust
- Local proof (area names, Google reviews)
- "Your neighbour" energy

### Digital Marketing / Courses
- Results-focused, data-heavy
- Show the method, not just the outcome
- Community and support emphasis
- "Successful peer" energy

### Professional Services (Consulting, Legal, Financial)
- More formal but still human
- Authority and expertise signals
- Case studies over testimonials
- "Trusted advisor" energy

### E-commerce / Physical Products
- Visual, sensory language
- Social proof volume matters
- Shipping and guarantee focus
- "Enthusiastic recommender" energy
