# Anti-Slop Profile: Local Boiler / Heating Engineer Website Copy (UK)

Derived from 30 generated samples (`unslop-output/analysis.md`). This file lists the
defaults the model collapses toward for this domain. Avoid them. The point is not to
adopt one new template — it is to stop recycling these.

## How to use
Before shipping any boiler/heating-engineer copy, grep it for the phrases below. Each one
you find is a place where you sounded like every competitor. Replace assertions with
demonstrations, and recycled phrases with the specific truth of *this* business.

## Phrases to avoid (recycled across nearly every sample)
- **"Gas Safe registered" as a trust flourish** (27/30). It's a legal baseline every rival
  states identically — it differentiates nothing. State it once as fact; never lean on it
  as the reason to choose you.
- **"Fixed prices, no surprises" / "no hidden costs" / "no shock invoices"** (17/30). The
  pricing-reassurance formula. If you can't publish prices, explain the *mechanism*
  ("you'll know the cost before I do anything chargeable") — don't chant the cliché.
- **"Honest" / "honest advice" / "honest quote" / "straight dealing"** as a character claim
  (16/30). Honesty asserted is worthless; honesty *demonstrated* is everything. Show the
  structural reason you won't rip them off (e.g. "I don't fit boilers, so I've no reason to
  push a replacement") instead of using the word.
- **"In plain English" / "no jargon"** (12/30). Don't claim plainness — *be* plain. Delete
  the meta-claim and just explain the thing simply.
- **"No call centre" / "you'll speak to a real person, not a machine"** (12/30). Every
  "local" firm says this in the same words.
- **"Sorted" / "get it sorted"** as the resolution verb (12/30).
- **"Peace of mind"** (8/30), **"no fuss"** (7/30), **"done properly"** (11/30),
  **"straightforward"** (10/30), **"clean and tidy / left tidy"** (11/30) — empty quality
  signals. Cut or replace with a concrete detail.
- **"Boiler packed in"** (7/30) and **January / "coldest day of the year"** failure framing
  (9/30) — the default seasonal-dread cliché.

## Sentence structures to avoid
- **Rhetorical pain-point opener**: "Boiler broken? No hot water?" (13/30). Effective once,
  but it's the automatic default — vary it, make the symptom specific, don't repeat it on
  every page's hero.
- **Fear-then-reassure hero**: problem sentence + "we'll sort it" sentence (16/30).
- **"Whether X or Y…"** spanning two customer scenarios (9/30) — a structural crutch.
- **Em-dash differentiator "X — not Y"** (14/30), usually "local — not a national call
  centre." Overused contrast.
- **Tricolon of negatives "No X. No Y. No Z."** (11/30).
- **"You can actually [rely on / trust]…"** authenticity tic (7/30).

## Structural defaults to avoid (the template every sample reaches for)
Hero (problem + city name) → trust bar of 4–5 badges → "Why Choose Us" (4–6 bold points) →
numbered "How It Works" (3–6 steps) → three testimonials → FAQ block → closing CTA (phone +
form fallback + reassurance line). 14/30 followed this almost identically. Breaking the
order, or earning each section instead of including it by reflex, is most of the fight.

## AI delivery tells (never let these reach the page)
- **"Here's the [content], ready to [action]"** opener (19/30).
- **Placeholder sign-off**: "Swap in your real phone number", "Before publishing, replace…"
  (22/30).
- **"Let me know if you'd like a different tone"** closer (13/30).
- Summary bullet list describing what was just written (8/30).

## What to do instead
Lead with the one specific, verifiable thing that is true only of this business — the
engineer's actual name and history, the exact symptom a caller is experiencing, the real
reason their incentives align with the customer's. Specificity is the only reliable
slop-killer; reassurance vocabulary is not.
