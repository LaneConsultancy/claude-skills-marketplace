# Radiator Installation and Replacement

**New radiator fitted, or an old one swapped out — for homeowners dealing with cold spots or adding heating to a room that doesn't have it.**

---

## When one room is colder than the rest

A single cold room is usually one of three things: an undersized radiator, an airlocked one, or a radiator that's silted up with years of sludge and barely circulates water anymore.

Bleeding and balancing can fix some of these. But if your radiator is 20 years old, visibly corroded, or still cold from the bottom up after bleeding, replacement is the faster fix. A new correctly-sized radiator — matched to the room's heat loss — will make a noticeable difference within a few hours of fitting.

---

## Adding heating to a new room

Loft conversions, extensions, and garden offices are the usual suspects here. The room gets built, and then the heating question gets asked.

Adding a radiator to an existing system involves running new pipework from the nearest suitable point on the circuit, fitting the radiator, and commissioning it into the system. If your boiler has the capacity, this is a straightforward job. If the system is already working close to its limit, we'll tell you before we start.

We size the radiator properly for the room — not just what fits on the wall.

---

## Replacing old or broken radiators

Some radiators are past saving. Persistent cold patches, visible rust on the exterior, or a slow leak around the valve are all signs a replacement makes more sense than another repair.

Modern radiators transfer heat more efficiently than older cast-iron or pressed-steel units, so the room heats up faster with less time on the boiler. If you're upgrading multiple radiators, it's worth doing them at the same time as a system flush — old sludge circulates into new radiators and degrades them quicker than it should.

---

## How the job works

1. **Survey** — we check the existing pipework, valves, and system pressure before quoting. No surprises on the day.
2. **Fitting** — the old radiator comes off, new one goes on. Valves replaced as needed.
3. **Balancing** — the system gets balanced so the new radiator heats evenly and doesn't affect other rooms.
4. **Test** — we run the system up to temperature and check for leaks before we leave.

Most single-radiator swaps take half a day. Adding a radiator to a new room depends on how far the pipework needs to run — we'll give you a time estimate when we quote.

## Common questions

**Can I just replace a radiator myself?**
You can swap like-for-like on the same valves without touching the system pressure, but the moment you're extending pipework or altering the circuit, that work should be done by a qualified heating engineer. Poorly balanced systems cause cold spots elsewhere and can shorten boiler life.

**Do I need a system flush if I'm only replacing one radiator?**
Not always. If the system water runs clean and the other radiators are heating evenly, you can usually get away without one. If the water looks dark or there's visible sludge on the old radiator, a powerflush before fitting is worth it.

**How do I know what size radiator I need?**
Room size is part of it, but heat loss through walls, windows, and insulation matters more. A 4m × 4m room with single glazing and no insulation needs a significantly larger radiator than the same room double-glazed and insulated. We work this out before we order anything.

**Can you fit a designer or column radiator?**
Yes. The plumbing is the same — only the flow rates and valve types sometimes differ. If you're buying your own radiator, send us the spec sheet and we'll confirm compatibility before the job.
