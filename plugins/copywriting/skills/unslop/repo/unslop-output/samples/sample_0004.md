**His Story**

I spent years working for British Gas. Good training, no question about it — but the longer I was there, the more I noticed a gap between what customers were being charged and what the job actually warranted. Pricing was opaque. Engineers were stretched thin. And homeowners were left feeling like they'd been processed, not helped.

So I left.

Going solo wasn't the easy option. But it meant I could do things the way I thought they should be done: turn up on time, explain what's wrong in plain English, and tell you the price before I start — not after.

That's not a sales pitch. It's just how I was brought up to treat people.
