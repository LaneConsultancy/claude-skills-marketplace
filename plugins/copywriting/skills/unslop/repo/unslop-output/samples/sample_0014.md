Here are three testimonial templates a heating engineer could hand to satisfied customers:

---

**Template 1 — Short & punchy (for Google reviews)**

> We called [Name] when our boiler stopped working on a [morning/evening]. He/she arrived [same day / next morning], diagnosed the fault quickly, and had it running again within [X hours]. Tidy worker, straight-talking about costs, no surprises on the invoice. Would use again without hesitation.

*Prompts to give the customer:* What was the job? How fast did they respond? Was the price what they expected?

---

**Template 2 — New installation (for website or case study)**

> We'd been putting off replacing our old combi for years. [Name] came out, had a look, and gave us a clear recommendation without trying to oversell. The installation took [one day / two days] and the difference is noticeable — the house heats up faster and our bills have already dropped. Everything was left clean and tidy. Genuinely pleased we went with [Company Name].

*Prompts to give the customer:* Old system vs new, how long it took, anything that surprised them positively.

---

**Template 3 — Longer, trust-building (for homepage or Checkatrade)**

> I've had a few heating engineers over the years and the difference with [Name] was noticeable from the start. He/she explained what was wrong, why it happened, and what my options were — including the cheaper one. The work itself was done on [date], took [X hours], and everything has been fine since. [Name] also pointed out [small issue — e.g. a dodgy valve, a poorly fitted flue] that the previous engineer had missed. Honest, knowledgeable, and easy to deal with. I've already passed his/her number to two neighbours.

*Prompts to give the customer:* Was anything explained clearly? Did they notice anything else that needed attention? Have they recommended you?

---

Each template is a starting point — tell customers to change any detail that doesn't fit, so the review reads like their own words rather than a form.
