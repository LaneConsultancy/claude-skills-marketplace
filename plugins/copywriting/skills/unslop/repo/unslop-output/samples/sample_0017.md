**FAQ**

**How long does a boiler installation take?**
Most installations are completed in a single day — typically 4 to 8 hours depending on whether we're replacing an existing boiler or installing from scratch. If pipework needs moving or a new flue route is required, it may run into a second day. We'll confirm the expected duration before we start.

**Do I need to be home during the installation?**
Yes. An adult needs to be present throughout the job to give access, sign off on completion, and receive a walkthrough of the new controls. We'll give you a morning or afternoon slot so you're not waiting around all day.

**What type of boiler do I need — combi, system, or conventional?**
It depends on how your home uses hot water. Combi boilers are the most popular choice for smaller homes with one bathroom — they heat water on demand with no storage tank. System boilers suit larger homes with higher hot water demand. Conventional (heat-only) boilers are mostly found in older properties with existing tanks in the loft. We'll assess your property and recommend the right type before quoting.

**Will you take away the old boiler?**
Yes, old boiler removal and disposal is included in the price. We'll also remove any redundant parts if you're switching system types.

**Do you offer finance?**
Yes. We work with several finance providers and can spread the cost over 12 to 60 months. Subject to credit approval — we'll go through the options with you when you book.

**Is the installation covered by a warranty?**
The boiler itself carries a manufacturer's warranty — typically 5 to 10 years depending on the brand and model. Our labour is guaranteed for 12 months. We'll register the boiler with the manufacturer on your behalf on the day.

**Will my gas supply need to be turned off?**
Yes, for the duration of the installation. We'll let you know in advance so you can plan around it. Water will also be off for part of the job.

**Do I need a new thermostat or smart controls?**
Not necessarily, but many older thermostats aren't compatible with newer boilers and a replacement is often worth it. Smart controls (like Nest or Hive) can cut energy bills noticeably over time. We'll tell you what's compatible with your new boiler and give you the option — no pressure either way.
